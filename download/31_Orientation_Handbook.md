## PURPOSE

This handbook covers everything you need to know before the Crucible begins. It is delivered on Day 1 (Orientation Day) and reviewed in the afternoon briefing sessions. Read it that night. The Crucible starts at 06:00 the day after tomorrow, and there will be no time to catch up.

---

## SECTION 1: THE XCITIZEN IDENTITY

### Who You Are Now

You are no longer an applicant, a candidate, or a "fellow." You are an **Xcitizen** — a deployed operator in the Hansa League. This is not a title; it is a function. You deploy, you build, you operate. You do not attend, study, or participate.

### What This Means Practically

| You ARE | You ARE NOT |
|---------|-------------|
| A deployed operator | An employee |
| Mission-deployed personnel | A student |
| A Pod member | A solo founder |
| A Commons contributor | An IP owner |
| Subject to the Gate System | Subject to performance reviews |
| Bound by the Supremacy Clause | Free to build your own thing |
| Here for 24 months | Here until you "find something better" |

### The XCitizen Mindset

1. **Execution over analysis.** When in doubt, act. Report what happened. Adjust.
2. **The Pod over the self.** Your success is the Pod's success. Your failure is the Pod's failure.
3. **The Commons over the individual.** What you learn belongs to everyone. What everyone learns belongs to you.
4. **The Gate over the politics.** The numbers are the numbers. Don't argue; don't lobby; don't spin.
5. **The cliff over the career.** You are not building a résumé. You are building infrastructure.

## SECTION 2: THE XEMBASSY

### What Is an XEmbassy?

An XEmbassy is a physical node in the Hansa network. It is part campus, part workshop, part community center. Your XEmbassy ([city]) is your base of operations for the Crucible and the operational hub for your deployment.

### Facilities (varies by XEmbassy type — M1/M2/M3)

| Facility | What it is | When you'll use it |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Commons** | Coworking + dining + social space | Daily (meals, work, social) |
| **Studio** | Software/design workspace | During ProtoCo build phases |
| **Lab** | R&D / testing workspace | If your ProtoCo requires R&D |
| **Foundry** | Manufacturing / prototyping workspace | If your ProtoCo requires hardware |
| **Outpost** | Last-mile deployment staging | During pilot and expansion phases |
| **Medical station** | On-site medical support | As needed; Neural Link monitoring hub |
| **Sleeping quarters** | Emergency rest rooms | During The Wall (if needed) |

### XEmbassy Rules

1. **Access:** Your badge gets you in 24/7. During the Crucible, you'll be there 06:00–21:00 daily.
2. **Safety:** Follow all safety protocols (foundry, lab, electrical). Report hazards to the Hub Manager immediately.
3. **Equipment:** Sign out shared equipment. Return it clean and functional. You are responsible for loss/damage.
4. **Cleanliness:** Clean up after yourself. The commons is shared by 1,000+ Xcitizens.
5. **Security:** Do not let non-Xcitizens into the XEmbassy. Do not share your access badge.
6. **Quiet hours:** 22:00–05:30 in the commons. No exceptions during the Crucible.

## SECTION 3: THE POD SYSTEM

### Your Pod

You will meet your Pod on Crucible Day 1. Until then, you don't know who they are. This is intentional — first impressions matter, and the Crucible is designed to create them authentically.

Your Pod is 10 Xcitizens, each assigned a different Archetype. You will build your ProtoCo together for the next 24 months. You succeed or fail together (the Gate is Pod-level).

### The Pod Leader

Every Pod has a Pod Leader — a senior XCitizen who has completed at least one deployment. Your Pod Leader is:
- Your direct supervisor for operations
- Your mentor for development
- Your advocate (and assessor) for Gate reviews
- Not a member of your Pod (they lead, they don't build)

Your Pod Leader's word is operational law within the Pod. If you disagree, you raise it privately. In the field, you execute.

### How Pods Operate

- **Daily standup:** 15 minutes, async on Slack. What did you do, what are you doing, what's blocked.
- **Weekly review:** 60 minutes with the Pod Leader. Progress vs. Gate trajectory.
- **Weekly Commons contribution:** Every Friday, the Pod submits learnings to the League Commons.
- **Decision-making:** Pod-level decisions are made by the Pilot (with input from all). Strategic Command decisions are non-negotiable.

## SECTION 4: THE LEAGUE COMMONS

### What It Is

The shared IP, infrastructure, and intelligence of the entire Hansa network. Every breakthrough, every failure, every Playbook, every vendor contact — all in one accessible pool.

### Your Access

You have full read/write access during deployment. You can:
- Search the Commons for solutions to your ProtoCo's problems
- Read prior cohorts' Playbooks (if your Field has them)
- Access vendor lists, regulatory guides, and partner networks
- Download infrastructure templates (legal, financial, operational)

### Your Contribution

You must contribute weekly:
- **Learnings:** What worked, what didn't, what you'd do differently (submit every Friday)
- **Breakthroughs:** Process improvements, technical solutions, supply chain wins (submit as they happen)
- **The Playbook:** At Gate 3, your Pod's codified operational manual becomes Commons IP

### Attribution

The Commons may attribute contributions to you by name. This is recognition, not ownership. The IP belongs to the League (Supremacy Clause).

## SECTION 5: THE GATE SYSTEM (PREVIEW)

The full Gate criteria are in Document 25 (Fellowship Agreement) and Document 46 (Gate Criteria). Here's what you need to know before the Crucible:

### The Five Gates

| Gate | When | What's Tested |
|------|------|---------------|
| Gate 0 | End of Crucible (Week 8) | Can you deploy? (Crucible certification) |
| Gate 1 | Month 6 | Did you build and deploy a Minimal Version? Did you get an Anchor Partner LOI? |
| Gate 2 | Month 12 | Are you generating revenue? Are your unit economics sustainable? |
| Gate 3 | Month 18 | Did you replicate in 3+ geographies? Did you author the Playbook? |
| Gate 4 | Month 24 | Can local operators run it at 80%+ efficiency? |

### Binary Outcomes

Pass = advance to the next phase.
Fail = Kill Switch (for Gates 1–3) or dissolution (Gate 4).

There is no partial credit. No "close enough." No appeals.

### What This Means for You

From Day 1 of the Crucible, everything you do is in service of passing Gates. The Crucible prepares you to deploy. Deployment Phase 1 is in service of Gate 1. And so on. Always know which Gate you're working toward.

## SECTION 6: THE NEURAL LINK PROTOCOL

### What It Is

A biometric monitoring device you wear 24/7 during deployment. It tracks:
- Heart rate variability (HRV)
- Sleep patterns (duration, quality)
- Stress indicators (galvanic skin response)
- Cognitive load proxies (activity patterns, response times)

### Why It Exists

1. **Pod performance optimization:** The system flags burnout risk before the Pod is aware of it, enabling preemptive intervention.
2. **Gate assessments:** Neural Link data is one input into Gate determinations (are you performing? is the Pod healthy?).
3. **Keeper/Cycled determination:** Cumulative Neural Link data is one factor in the Month 24 cliff determination.
4. **Medical safety:** During the Crucible (especially The Wall), the medical team monitors for dangerous physiological responses.

### Your Privacy

- Data is accessible to: Strategic Command, your medical/ops team, and you.
- Data is NOT accessible to: other Xcitizens, other Pods, external parties.
- You can request your data at any time.
- Aggregate, anonymized data may be used for research and algorithm improvement.

### Your Obligation

Wear it. 24/7. Charging time and medical exceptions are the only acceptable breaks. Going without it for more than 24 hours is a Conduct violation.

## SECTION 7: THE CLIFF (PREVIEW)

At Month 24, the deployment ends. The stipend stops. Outcomes:

- **Keepers (top 3%):** Permanent payroll in the spun-out entity. Salary, benefits, equity acceleration.
- **The Cycled (97%):** Severed with performance equity (36-month vesting). XCitizen status and network access retained.

This is not a failure. It is the design. The League builds ventures, not careers.

**Do not fixate on the 3%.** Fixating on Keeper status during the deployment is a distraction. Focus on the Gate in front of you. The Keeper determination takes care of itself if you operate well.

## SECTION 8: WHAT HAPPENS TOMORROW

Tomorrow at 06:00, the Crucible begins. Here's what to expect on Day 1:

1. **06:00 — Arrive at XEmbassy.** Commons area. Coffee provided. (This is the last caffeine you'll be glad about.)
2. **06:15 — Cohort formation.** You'll be directed to your Pod's designated area. This is the first time you meet your Pod.
3. **06:30 — Pod Leader introduction.** Your Pod Leader briefs the Pod on the Crucible rules and opens the ProtoCo commission brief.
4. **07:00 — The Crucible begins.** The first scenario. No further warning.

**What to bring on Day 1:**
- Your access badge
- Your Neural Link device (already wearing it)
- A notebook and pen
- A water bottle
- Comfortable, durable clothing
- Nothing else. No laptop. No phone (it will be held during scenarios).

**What not to bring:**
- Expectations about how this will go
- A plan for "how to win"
- Anything you think you know
