## PURPOSE

The Playbook is the codified operational manual for your ProtoCo. It is the Gate 3 deliverable (Month 18) and the Pod's most significant contribution to the League Commons. It must enable any future operator to deploy, operate, and scale the ProtoCo **without the original Pod's involvement.**

**Start writing the Playbook in Month 1.** Do not wait until Month 17. Add to it weekly. By Gate 3, it should be 50+ pages and pass the "outsider test" (an XCitizen from another Pod follows it for 1 day and successfully operates the ProtoCo).

---

## THE OUTSIDER TEST (GATE 3 REQUIREMENT)

At Gate 3, an XCitizen from another Pod (who has never seen your ProtoCo) must:
1. Read the Playbook
2. Follow it for 1 day without your Pod's assistance
3. Successfully operate the ProtoCo (users served, revenue collected, no critical errors)

If they can't, the Playbook fails. Gate 3 fails. Kill Switch.

**The Playbook is not a document. It is a transfer of operational capability.** If it doesn't transfer, it's not a Playbook — it's a manual that nobody can use.

## PLAYBOOK STRUCTURE

The Playbook must cover the following 10 sections. Each section is a chapter. Minimum 50 pages total.

### Chapter 1: Setup — How to Stand Up the ProtoCo in a New Geography

**Purpose:** Enable a new operator to deploy the ProtoCo from scratch in a new location.

**Contents:**
1.1 Site selection criteria (what makes a good deployment site)
1.2 Site preparation (space, power, water, connectivity, security requirements)
1.3 Equipment list (everything needed, with specs and sourcing)
1.4 Procurement guide (where to buy, how long it takes, what it costs)
1.5 Installation step-by-step (with photos/diagrams)
1.6 Configuration and calibration (initial setup parameters)
1.7 Testing protocol (how to verify it works before users arrive)
1.8 User recruitment (how to find and onboard the first users)
1.9 Launch checklist (everything that must be done before "go live")

**Template:**
```
## 1.[X] [Sub-process]

**Purpose:** [Why this step matters]
**Time required:** [X hours/days]
**Materials needed:** [list]
**Who does it:** [Role — e.g., "Builder + Operator"]

### Step-by-step
1. [Step 1 — with photo/diagram]
2. [Step 2]
3. ...

### Common mistakes
- [Mistake 1] — [How to avoid/fix]
- [Mistake 2] — [How to avoid/fix]

### Quality check
[How to verify this step was done correctly]
```

## PLAYBOOK WRITING RULES

### 1. Write for a Stranger
The reader is someone who has never seen your ProtoCo. They don't know your jargon, your shortcuts, or your assumptions. Explain everything. If you find yourself writing "obviously," delete it and explain instead.

### 2. Photos and Diagrams
Every physical procedure needs photos or diagrams. Text alone is insufficient. Take photos during Q1 and Q2 — you won't have time in Q3.

### 3. Be Specific
"Check the system" is not a step. "Check that the pressure gauge reads between 2.0 and 3.0 bar" is a step. Specific, measurable, verifiable.

### 4. Include Failures
Document what goes wrong, not just what goes right. "Common mistakes" sections are the most valuable parts of the Playbook. Future operators will make the same mistakes — help them avoid or fix them.

### 5. Test as You Write
Don't write a procedure and assume it works. Have a Pod member (who didn't write it) follow the procedure step-by-step while you watch. Where do they get stuck? What's unclear? Fix it.

### 6. Version It
The Playbook is a living document. Version it (v1.0, v1.1, v2.0). Track what changed in each version and why. By Gate 3, you should be on v3.0+.

## THE OUTSIDER TEST PROTOCOL (MONTH 17)

1. **Identify 2-3 XCitizens from other Pods** (different Fields, different Archetypes — the more diverse, the better the test).
2. **Give them the Playbook** (no verbal explanation — just the document).
3. **They follow it for 1 day** at one of your deployment sites. Your Pod is present but does NOT help (you observe and take notes).
4. **At the end of the day:**
   - Did they successfully operate the ProtoCo? (users served, revenue collected, no critical errors)
   - Where did they get stuck?
   - What was unclear?
   - What was missing?
5. **Fix the Playbook** based on their feedback. Re-test if necessary.
6. **Document the test results** — they are Gate 3 evidence.

**If the outsider cannot operate the ProtoCo, the Playbook is not ready.** Fix it. Re-test. The Gate 3 assessment includes the outsider test results.

## POST-GATE 3: THE PLAYBOOK AS COMMONS IP

After Gate 3, the Playbook becomes League Commons IP. It is accessible to all future Pods and ventures. Your Pod's name is attributed (recognition, not ownership).

The Playbook is your legacy. The venture you built will be operated by people you never meet, in geographies you never visit, using the Playbook you wrote. Make it worthy of that responsibility.
