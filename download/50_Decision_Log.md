# 50. DECISION LOG

**xHansa Fellowship — Pod Decision Log**
**Pod [ID] | ProtoCo: [Name]**

---

## PURPOSE

The Decision Log is the Pod's institutional memory. Every significant decision is recorded here — what was decided, why, by whom, with what input, and what the outcome was. This ensures:
1. **Accountability:** Decisions are traceable to a decision-maker and a rationale.
2. **Learning:** When a decision leads to a good or bad outcome, the Pod can review why.
3. **Continuity:** If a member leaves, the Pod knows why past decisions were made.
4. **Gate evidence:** The Decision Log is part of the Gate submission (it shows operational discipline).

The Comms maintains the Decision Log in Notion. All members can read it. The Pilot reviews it weekly.

---

## WHAT TO LOG

Log decisions that are:
- **Strategic:** Changes to the ProtoCo's direction, target market, pricing, or scope
- **Financial:** Budget allocations > $500, vendor contracts, pricing changes
- **Operational:** Process changes, tool adoption, team role adjustments
- **Partnership:** Anchor Partner selection, vendor selection, customer commitments
- **Risk:** Risk acceptance (deciding to proceed despite a flagged risk)
- **Gate-related:** Decisions that affect Gate readiness

Do NOT log:
- Routine operational tasks (the sprint tracker covers those)
- Minor tactical adjustments (use the meeting notes)
- Personal decisions (not the Pod's business)

---

## THE LOG TEMPLATE

Each entry uses this format:

```
### Decision [Number]: [Title]
**Date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Decided by:** [Pilot name]
**Decision:** [What was decided — one sentence]

**Context:** [Why this decision was needed — 2-3 sentences]

**Input received:**
- [Member name] ([Archetype]): [Their input — one sentence]
- [Member name] ([Archetype]): [Their input]
- ...

**Dissent:** [Any disagreement, and how it was resolved — or "None"]

**Rationale:** [Why this option over alternatives — 2-3 sentences]

**Expected outcome:** [What we expect to happen — one sentence]

**Review date:** [When to revisit this decision, if applicable — or "N/A"]

**Status:** [Active / Superseded by Decision #___ / Reversed by Decision #___]
```

## SAMPLE ENTRIES

### Decision 001: Minimal Version Scope — Water Purification Unit (Not Full System)

**Date:** 2025-08-15
**Decided by:** [Pilot name]
**Decision:** The Minimal Version will be a single-unit water purification device (capacity: 500L/day), not a multi-unit municipal system.

**Context:** The ProtoCo commission specifies "decentralized water purification for arid zones." The Pod debated whether to build a single household unit or a multi-unit community system. The Gate 1 deadline (Month 6) requires a deployed Minimal Version — a multi-unit system is too complex to build and deploy in 5 months.

**Input received:**
- [Builder 1]: "A single unit is buildable in 3 months. A multi-unit system needs 8+ months."
- [Hustler]: "The Anchor Partner (NGO) wants a community-level solution. A single unit may not satisfy them."
- [Operator]: "A single unit is easier to maintain and deploy in the field."
- [Tracker]: "Risk: the Anchor Partner may reject a single-unit solution as 'not what we asked for.'"
- [Comms]: "We can frame the single unit as 'Phase 1 of a community system' in the LOI conversation."

**Dissent:** [Hustler] expressed concern that the Anchor Partner would reject the scope. Resolved by: the Comms will frame it as Phase 1 in the LOI conversation, and the Hustler will test the framing with the Anchor Partner before the formal LOI ask.

**Rationale:** The Gate 1 deadline is non-negotiable. A multi-unit system cannot be built and deployed in 5 months. A single unit satisfies the "decentralized" requirement, is buildable, and can be framed as Phase 1. The risk (Anchor Partner rejection) is manageable through communication.

**Expected outcome:** Single-unit Minimal Version built and deployed by Month 5. Anchor Partner LOI secured by Month 6 (Gate 1).

**Review date:** 2025-09-15 (after Anchor Partner LOI conversation — if rejected, revisit scope)

**Status:** Active

### Decision 002: Vendor Selection — Solar Panel Supplier

**Date:** 2025-09-02
**Decided by:** [Pilot name]
**Decision:** The Pod will source solar panels from [Vendor A] (Nairobi-based, $2.50/panel, 4-week lead time) rather than [Vendor B] (Dubai-based, $1.80/panel, 8-week lead time).

**Context:** The Minimal Version requires 20 solar panels. Two vendors were identified. Vendor B is cheaper but has a longer lead time. The Gate 1 deadline is tight.

**Input received:**
- [Builder 2]: "Vendor A's panels are slightly lower efficiency (16% vs 18%) but the difference doesn't matter for our use case."
- [Operator]: "Vendor A is local — easier logistics, faster replacement if a panel fails."
- [Tracker]: "Risk: Vendor A is a smaller supplier. If they can't fulfill, we lose 4 weeks. Mitigation: order 2 extra panels as buffer."
- [Hustler]: "Vendor B's cost savings ($14 total) are negligible vs the risk of missing the Gate 1 deadline."

**Dissent:** None.

**Rationale:** The 4-week lead time advantage is critical for the Gate 1 deadline. The cost difference ($14) is negligible. Local sourcing simplifies logistics and maintenance. The Tracker's risk (supplier failure) is mitigated by ordering buffer panels.

**Expected outcome:** Panels delivered by 2025-09-30. Minimal Version assembly begins 2025-10-01.

**Review date:** N/A (operational decision)

**Status:** Active

## LOG MAINTENANCE RULES

1. **Log within 24 hours.** Decisions are recorded the day they're made. Don't batch — memory degrades.
2. **The Comms owns the log.** They draft entries. The Pilot reviews for accuracy.
3. **Number sequentially.** Decision 001, 002, 003... Never reuse numbers.
4. **Update status.** If a decision is superseded or reversed, update the status field (don't delete the entry — the history matters).
5. **Review weekly.** In the Weekly Review (Friday), the Comms presents new decisions. The Pod confirms accuracy.
6. **Gate submission.** At each Gate, the Decision Log is included as evidence of operational discipline.

---

## QUARTERLY DECISION REVIEW

At each Gate (Months 6, 12, 18, 24), the Pod reviews the Decision Log:
- **Which decisions led to good outcomes?** (Learn from success.)
- **Which decisions led to bad outcomes?** (Learn from failure.)
- **Which decisions would we make differently?** (Feed into the Commons contribution.)
- **Are any decisions still active that should be revisited?** (Update review dates.)

This review is part of the Gate submission (it shows the Pod is learning, not just executing).
