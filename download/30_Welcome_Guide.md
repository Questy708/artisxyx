## WELCOME TO THE HANSA

You made it. You read the terms, you passed the Typing Assessment, you survived the acceptance process, and you signed the agreements. Now you're here — about to begin 24 months that will be the hardest and most important of your life.

This guide is the first thing you'll read on arrival. It tells you what happens in the first 72 hours, where to go, who to talk to, and what to expect. Keep it on your phone.

## YOUR FIRST 72 HOURS

### Day 0 — Arrival Day (3 days before Crucible)

**When you land:**
- A League driver will meet you at the airport (look for the "XC" sign). If you don't see them, call [Hub Manager phone].
- You'll be driven to your housing (shared housing near XEmbassy [city]).

**When you arrive at housing:**
- Check in with the Housing Coordinator (on-site).
- Collect your room key, access badge, and welcome packet (this guide + maps + schedule).
- Your room will have: bed, desk, locker, Wi-Fi credentials, and a printed copy of the Participant Handbook.
- Rest. You've traveled. Tomorrow starts.

**Evening:**
- Optional: walk to the XEmbassy (15 min walk / 5 min ride) to orient yourself. The commons area is open. You'll meet other arriving Xcitizens informally.
- Sleep early. The Crucible starts in 3 days and you need every hour of rest you can bank.

### Day 1 — Orientation Day (2 days before Crucible)

**08:00 — Breakfast at XEmbassy commons**
- Breakfast is provided. Eat well. This is your routine for the next 8 weeks.

**09:00 — XEmbassy orientation**
- Hub Manager gives a tour: commons, workspace, foundry, lab, studio, medical station, emergency exits.
- You'll receive your XEmbassy access credentials (badge, Wi-Fi, lab/foundry authorization).
- Safety briefing: fire, medical, security protocols.

**10:30 — Administrative check-in**
- Submit signed agreements (if not already submitted digitally).
- Confirm stipend setup (bank details, first payment date).
- Submit emergency contact and medical information forms.
- Receive your Neural Link device (don't put it on yet — that happens at the medical screening).

**12:00 — Lunch at commons**

**13:00 — Medical screening**
- Baseline biometrics: heart rate, blood pressure, blood panel.
- Neural Link device fitting and activation. You'll wear it from this point forward (24/7).
- Medical history review (confidential, accessible only to the medical team).

**15:00 — League Commons briefing**
- What the Commons is, how to access it, how to contribute.
- Your Commons account credentials.
- Weekly contribution expectations.

**16:00 — Gate System briefing**
- The five Gates, the criteria, the Kill Switch.
- Your ProtoCo commission brief (sealed — open it with your Pod on Crucible Day 1).
- Q&A with a Strategic Command representative.

**17:00 — Free time**
- Walk around. Meet other Xcitizens. The Pod rosters haven't been distributed yet — you won't know who your Pod is until Crucible Day 1.

**19:00 — Welcome dinner**
- Cohort-wide dinner at the commons. Casual. No speeches. Just food and people.

### Day 2 — Rest + Prep (1 day before Crucible)

**Morning:**
- Sleep in. Eat well. Walk. Decompress.

**Afternoon:**
- Optional: TownSquare setup. Create your profile, join your Field community, introduce yourself.
- Optional: review the Participant Handbook (Document 29).
- Optional: organize your personal affairs (family contacts, personal admin) — you won't have much time for this once the Crucible starts.

**Evening:**
- **Sleep early.** The Crucible begins tomorrow at 06:00.
- Set multiple alarms. Late arrivals are not admitted to the Crucible.

### Day 3 — Crucible Day 1

**05:30 — Wake up.** Eat a real breakfast. Hydrate.

**06:00 — Arrive at XEmbassy.** The Crucible begins. You'll meet your Pod for the first time. Your Pod Leader will be introduced. The ProtoCo commission briefs will be opened.

**From this point forward, your life belongs to the deployment.** The next 8 weeks are the Crucible. The next 24 months are the deployment. Welcome.

## WHAT TO EXPECT IN THE CRUCIBLE (PREVIEW)

The full Crucible Handbook is Document 39. Here's the preview:

- **Weeks 1–5: "The Shock."** Systematic destabilization. Ambiguous tasks, shifting constraints, impossible deadlines. You'll be stripped of the habit of waiting for permission and forced into execution-first decision-making.
- **Weeks 4–5: "The Wall."** Sustained cognitive exhaustion. Sleep restricted to 5–6 hours. Relentless pace for 10–14 days. This is the phase that separates those who can operate when spent from those who cannot.
- **Weeks 6–7: Reconstruction.** The pace eases. You're taught the xCelero operating model. You begin scoping your ProtoCo. Archetype assignments finalize.
- **Week 8: Gate 0.** Certification. Pass → deploy. Fail → recycle or release.

It will be the hardest thing you've ever done. It is designed to be. If it weren't, it wouldn't filter.

## THE UNWRITTEN RULES

These aren't in the agreements. They're what experienced Xcitizens and alumni would tell you if you asked:

1. **Sleep when you can.** During The Wall, every hour counts. Don't stay up socializing.
2. **Eat.** The food is there. Eat it. Skipping meals to "push through" is not heroism — it's self-sabotage.
3. **Ask for help early.** The medical team would rather hear "I'm struggling" on Day 3 than "I'm broken" on Day 14.
4. **Don't compete with your Pod.** You succeed or fail together. The Gate is Pod-level.
5. **Contribute to the Commons immediately.** Don't wait until you have something "good enough." Contribute what you learn today, even if it's a failure.
6. **Write things down.** The Reflection Journal (Document 43) is not optional. You'll need it for Gate assessments.
7. **Be honest in your debriefs.** The Crucible instructors can tell when you're performing. They're assessing execution, not presentation.
8. **Respect the process.** You may not understand why a scenario is designed the way it is. That's intentional. Understanding comes later.

## IF YOU'RE HOMESICK, SCARED, OR DOUBTING

This is normal. The Crucible is designed to create these feelings. You are not the first to feel them and you won't be the last.

- **Talk to your Pod Leader.** They've been through it.
- **Talk to the medical team.** Confidential. Non-judgmental.
- **Write in your Reflection Journal.** Get it out of your head and onto paper.
- **Remember why you're here.** You read the terms. You accepted the deal. You chose to build infrastructure for the Global South. That choice is being tested right now. That's the point.
