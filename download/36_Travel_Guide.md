# 36. TRAVEL GUIDE

**xHansa Fellowship — Travel Guide**

---

## 1. OVERVIEW

During your 24-month deployment, you will travel — to your XEmbassy, to field deployment sites, to partner meetings, and potentially to other XEmbassies for replication (Q3). The League covers all deployment-related travel. This guide covers everything you need to know.

---

## 2. INITIAL TRAVEL TO THE XEMBASSY

### 2.1 What the League Covers
- **Flights:** Economy class, from your home city to the XEmbassy city. Booked by the Logistics Coordinator.
- **Ground transport:** Airport pickup (a League driver meets you) and drop-off at housing.
- **Visas:** Application fees and processing (if required). The Logistics Coordinator assists but you provide documents.
- **Excess baggage:** One extra bag (up to 23kg) for personal belongings. Anything beyond that is at your expense.
- **Travel insurance:** Emergency medical + travel insurance for the journey.

### 2.2 What You Handle
- Valid passport (6+ months validity from deployment start date)
- Visa application documents (the Logistics Coordinator tells you what's needed)
- Personal items for the flight
- Getting to your home-city departure airport

### 2.3 Timeline

| When | What |
|------|------|
| Day -14 | Logistics Coordinator contacts you to arrange travel |
| Day -10 | Flight booked; visa application submitted (if needed) |
| Day -5 | Visa confirmed (if needed); flight details sent to you |
| Day -3 | You fly to the XEmbassy city |
| Day -3 | League driver picks you up at the airport; taken to housing |
| Day -2 | Orientation Day 1 (see Welcome Guide, Doc 30) |
| Day -1 | Rest + Prep Day (see Welcome Guide) |
| Day 0 | Crucible begins at 06:00 |

### 2.4 What to Pack for the Flight
See the Packing Guide (Document 38) for the full list. For the flight specifically:
- Passport + visa (printed copy)
- Acceptance letter (printed, in case of immigration questions)
- xCelero emergency contact (Hub Manager phone, printed)
- Change of clothes (in case checked baggage is delayed)
- Personal medications (in carry-on, not checked)
- Phone + charger
- A book (you'll want the mental rest before the Crucible)

## 3. FIELD DEPLOYMENT TRAVEL (PHASES 1–4)

### 3.1 What Triggers Travel
- **Q1 (Months 1–6):** Travel to the pilot deployment site (the geography where your Minimal Version will be deployed). May be a single site or multiple.
- **Q2 (Months 7–12):** Travel to customer sites, partner meetings, and the pilot site for maintenance/check-ins.
- **Q3 (Months 13–18):** Travel to 3–5 new replication geographies. This is the most travel-intensive phase.
- **Q4 (Months 19–24):** Travel to train local operators and hand over operations. Decreasing as local operators take over.

### 3.2 Booking Process

1. **Identify the need:** Your Pod determines a travel need (site visit, partner meeting, replication trip).
2. **Submit a travel request:** In the venture tracker, submit a Travel Request with: destination, dates, purpose, estimated cost, who's traveling.
3. **Pod Leader approval:** Your Pod Leader approves (or escalates to Strategic Command if cost > $2,000).
4. **Logistics booking:** The Logistics Coordinator books flights, accommodation, and ground transport within 48 hours of approval.
5. **Pre-travel briefing:** Your Pod Leader briefs you on objectives, contacts, and safety.

### 3.3 What the League Covers

| Expense | Covered? | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Flights (economy) | ✅ | Booked by Logistics |
| Ground transport (taxi, ride-hail, rental) | ✅ | Pre-approved; receipt required |
| Accommodation | ✅ | Mid-range hotels or League-arranged housing |
| Meals (per diem) | ✅ | $30/day per diem (receipts not required for per diem) |
| Visas (if needed) | ✅ | For deployment countries |
| Travel insurance | ✅ | Included in League coverage |
| Personal expenses (souvenirs, entertainment) | ❌ | At your expense |
| Upgrades (business class, luxury hotel) | ❌ | At your expense |
| Travel outside deployment countries | ❌ | At your expense (unless Strategic Command approves) |

### 3.4 Per Diem
$30/day for meals and incidentals. Paid as a flat rate (no receipts needed) on your next stipend payment. This is in addition to your regular stipend.

### 3.5 Receipt Policy
- **Flights, accommodation, visas:** Booked directly by Logistics — no receipts needed from you.
- **Ground transport, meals over per diem, other reimbursables:** Submit receipts via the venture tracker within 7 days. Reimbursement on next stipend payment.
- **Lost receipts:** If you lose a receipt, you can still claim up to $50 with a signed declaration. Over $50 without a receipt is not reimbursable.

## 4. TRAVEL SAFETY

### 4.1 Pre-Travel Checklist
- [ ] Destination research: political stability, health risks, security situation (check your government's travel advisory)
- [ ] Vaccinations: up to date for the destination (consult the medical team)
- [ ] Medications: sufficient supply for the trip duration
- [ ] Emergency contacts: Hub Manager, Pod Leader, local contact (if any) — saved offline
- [ ] Insurance: League travel insurance confirmed for the destination
- [ | Documents: passport (6+ months validity), visa (if required), acceptance letter (immigration), travel insurance card
- [ ] Communication: phone roaming activated or local SIM plan confirmed
- [ ] Neural Link: charged and wearing (it works internationally)

### 4.2 During Travel
- Share your itinerary with your Pod Leader and the Hub Manager
- Check in with your Pod on Slack upon arrival and departure
- Avoid high-risk areas (check travel advisories)
- Don't carry large amounts of cash (use cards where possible)
- Keep your passport secure (hotel safe when not needed)
- Don't discuss ProtoCo details in public spaces

### 4.3 Emergency Protocol
If you have a travel emergency (medical, security, lost documents):
1. **Immediate danger:** Call local emergency services first (112, 911, 999 — varies by country)
2. **Medical emergency:** Contact the medical team at medical@xcelero.com / [phone] — 24/7
3. **Security emergency:** Contact the Hub Manager at [phone] — 24/7
4. **Lost passport:** Contact your embassy + the Hub Manager
5. **Lost Neural Link device:** Contact the Hub Manager immediately — a replacement will be couriered

## 5. INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL (Q3 REPLICATION)

During Q3, you may travel to 3–5 different countries for replication. This is the most complex travel phase.

### 5.1 Multi-Country Visa Strategy
- The Logistics Coordinator helps identify visa requirements for each destination
- Some countries have e-visas (fast); others require embassy applications (slow — start early)
- If a visa is denied, the Pod Leader determines whether to find an alternative geography or adjust the replication plan

### 5.2 Cultural Awareness
- Research cultural norms for each destination (dress, greetings, business etiquette)
- The Commons has a "Country Guides" section with prior Xcitizens' notes on operating in specific countries
- When in doubt, ask your Pod Leader or a local contact

### 5.3 Health
- Check vaccination requirements for each country (yellow fever, malaria prophylaxis, etc.)
- The medical team provides pre-travel health briefings and prophylactics
- Food and water safety: drink bottled water in unfamiliar locations; eat at established restaurants

## 6. TRAVEL DURING THE CRUCIBLE

**No travel during the Crucible.** You are at the XEmbassy 06:00–21:00 daily for 8 weeks. The only exception is a medical emergency (handled by the medical team).

---

## 7. POST-DEPLOYMENT TRAVEL (POST-CLIFF)

After the cliff (Month 24):
- **Keepers:** Travel to the spun-out entity's HQ (arranged by the entity, not the League)
- **The Cycled:** Your League-funded travel ends. You arrange your own departure from the XEmbassy. The League covers one return flight home.
