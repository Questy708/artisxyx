# 52. MEETING TEMPLATES

**xHansa Fellowship — Pod Meeting Templates**

---

## PURPOSE

Standardized meeting templates ensure every meeting has a clear purpose, structure, and output. The Pod runs four recurring meetings. Use these templates every time. Do not improvise structure — consistency is a feature.

---

## 1. DAILY STANDUP (ASYNC, 5 MIN)

**When:** Every day by 08:00 (async on Slack)
**Duration:** 5 minutes (to write)
**Owner:** Each member posts their own

### Template
```
**Standup — [Date]**
✅ Yesterday: [1-2 sentences — what you did]
→ Today: [1-2 sentences — what you're doing]
🚧 Blocked: [What's blocking you, or "Nothing"]
```

### Rules
- Post by 08:00. Late posts are noted.
- Keep it short. If it's more than 3 lines, use a thread or schedule a call.
- "Blocked" means: I cannot proceed without help or a decision. If you can proceed but it's hard, that's not blocked — that's work.
- The Pod Leader reads every standup. If you're blocked, they help.

## 2. WEEKLY SPRINT REVIEW (SYNC, 60 MIN)

**When:** Every Friday (or day set by Pod Leader)
**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Owner:** Pilot runs it. Comms documents it.

### Agenda Template

```
## Weekly Sprint Review — Week [X] | [Date]

### 1. Sprint Goal Check (10 min)
**Sprint goal:** [Last week's goal — one sentence]
**Achieved?** Yes / No / Partially
**If no/partial:** What slipped? Why? Does it affect Gate trajectory?

### 2. Individual Reports (20 min, 2 min per member)
Each member reports:
- **What I did this week** (1-2 sentences)
- **What I'm doing next week** (1 sentence)
- **Blockers / risks** (1 sentence or "None")

### 3. Risk Review (10 min)
**Led by:** Tracker
- New risks identified this week: [list]
- Risks escalated from last week: [status]
- Risk Register updated: Yes / No

### 4. Commons Contribution (15 min)
**Led by:** Comms
- Draft contribution: [What worked / What didn't / What we'd do differently]
- Pod review and approval: [Approved / Revisions needed]

### 5. Next Week's Sprint Goal (5 min)
**Set by:** Pilot
- Sprint goal for next week: [One sentence]
- Key tasks by Archetype: [list]

### 6. Open Floor (5 min)
- Any concerns, questions, or announcements?

### Action Items
- [ ] [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]
- [ ] [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]
```

### Output
- Meeting notes posted to Pod Slack channel by end of day (Comms)
- Commons contribution posted to #commons-contributions by 18:00 (Comms)
- Risk Register updated in Notion (Tracker)
- Decision Log updated if any decisions were made (Comms)

## 3. MONTHLY STRATEGIC REVIEW (SYNC, 90 MIN)

**When:** Once per month (last Friday of the month)
**Duration:** 90 minutes
**Owner:** Pod Leader runs it. Comms documents it.

### Agenda Template

```
## Monthly Strategic Review — Month [X] | [Date]
**Attendees:** Pod (10) + Pod Leader
**Purpose:** Deep review of Gate trajectory, budget, risks, and team health.

### 1. Gate Trajectory Review (20 min)
**Next Gate:** Gate [N] in [X] weeks
**Gate criteria:** [List the specific criteria for this Gate]
**Current status:**
- Criterion 1: [On track / At risk / Behind] — [evidence]
- Criterion 2: [On track / At risk / Behind] — [evidence]
- ...
**Overall Gate readiness:** [Green / Yellow / Red]
**If Yellow/Red:** What's the recovery plan?

### 2. Budget Review (15 min)
**Led by:** Operator
**Budget burn this month:** $___
**Cumulative budget burn:** $___ of $___ total
**Variance:** [Under / Over] budget by $___
**Significant expenses:** [list]
**Forecast:** [Are we on budget for the phase?]

### 3. Risk Register Review (15 min)
**Led by:** Tracker
**Total active risks:** ___
**New risks this month:** [list]
**Risks resolved this month:** [list]
**Top 3 risks:**
1. [Risk] — [Severity] — [Mitigation status]
2. [Risk] — [Severity] — [Mitigation status]
3. [Risk] — [Severity] — [Mitigation status]

### 4. Team Health Review (15 min)
**Led by:** Pod Leader (with Neural Link data)
**Pod health summary:** [Green / Yellow / Red]
**Individual concerns (confidential):** [Any members showing burnout signals?]
**Action:** [Any interventions needed?]

### 5. Commons Contribution Review (10 min)
**Contributions this month:** [count]
**Quality:** [Are we contributing meaningful learnings?]
**Commons usage:** [Are we searching the Commons before solving problems?]
**Improvement:** [What can we do better?]

### 6. Pod Dynamics (10 min)
**Led by:** Pod Leader
**Conflicts:** [Any unresolved?]
**Communication:** [Healthy? Any breakdowns?]
**Morale:** [How is the Pod feeling?]

### 7. Next Month's Plan (5 min)
**Set by:** Pilot
**Monthly goal:** [One sentence — what must be true by the next Monthly Review]
**Key milestones:** [list]

### Action Items
- [ ] [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]
- [ ] [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]
```

### Output
- Meeting notes posted to Pod channel (comms)
- Monthly summary submitted to Strategic Command (Pod Leader)
- Budget tracker updated (Operator)
- Risk Register updated (Tracker)

## 4. GATE READINESS REVIEW (SYNC, 90 MIN)

**When:** 4 weeks before each Gate (Months 5, 11, 17, 23)
**Duration:** 90 minutes
**Owner:** Pilot runs it. Pod Leader attends.

### Agenda Template

```
## Gate [N] Readiness Review — [Date]
**Gate date:** [Date — 4 weeks from now]
**Gate criteria:** [List all criteria for this Gate]

### 1. Criteria-by-Criteria Assessment (30 min)
For each Gate criterion:
- **Criterion:** [description]
- **Current status:** [Done / In progress / Not started]
- **Evidence available:** [What evidence do we have? What do we need?]
- **Gap:** [What's missing?]
- **Action to close gap:** [What will we do in the next 4 weeks?]
- **Confidence:** [High / Medium / Low] that we'll pass this criterion

### 2. Risk Assessment (15 min)
**Led by:** Tracker
- What could cause us to fail this Gate?
- What's the probability and impact of each risk?
- What mitigations are in place?

### 3. Evidence Compilation Plan (15 min)
**Led by:** Comms
- What evidence needs to be submitted? [list]
- Who is responsible for compiling each piece? [assign]
- When will each piece be ready? [timeline]
- Format: [Photos, logs, data, documents, etc.]

### 4. Budget Check (10 min)
**Led by:** Operator
- Do we have budget to execute the 4-week plan? Yes / No
- If no: What do we need? [Escalate to Pod Leader]

### 5. Pod Health Check (10 min)
**Led by:** Pod Leader
- Is the Pod rested and healthy for the final push?
- Any burnout concerns?
- Any conflicts that need resolution before the Gate?

### 6. 4-Week Sprint Plan (10 min)
**Set by:** Pilot
- Week 1 (of 4): [Sprint goal]
- Week 2: [Sprint goal]
- Week 3: [Sprint goal — evidence compilation begins]
- Week 4: [Sprint goal — Gate submission]

### Action Items
- [ ] [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]
- [ ] [Task] — [Owner] — [Due date]
```

### Output
- 4-week sprint plan (all members)
- Evidence compilation assignments (Comms)
- Risk mitigation plan (Tracker)
- Gate readiness confidence: Green / Yellow / Red (Pilot determines, Pod Leader reviews)

## 5. AD-HOC MEETING TEMPLATE (SYNC, VARIABLE)

**When:** As needed (called by any member, approved by Pilot)
**Duration:** 15–45 minutes (time-boxed before starting)
**Owner:** The caller

### Template
```
## Ad-Hoc Meeting — [Topic] | [Date]
**Called by:** [Name]
**Duration:** [X] minutes (time-boxed)
**Purpose:** [One sentence — why this meeting is needed]
**Attendees:** [Who needs to be there — not the whole Pod unless necessary]

### Agenda
1. [Item 1] — [X min]
2. [Item 2] — [X min]
3. Decision needed: [What decision must be made by the end?]

### Output
- Decision: [What was decided]
- Action items: [list]
- Posted to: Pod channel (if relevant to whole Pod) or not (if specific)
```

### Rules
- **Time-boxed.** Set the duration before starting. End on time.
- **Purpose-driven.** If there's no decision or output, it should have been a Slack message.
- **Minimal attendees.** Only invite who needs to be there. Don't default to the whole Pod.
- **Documented.** If a decision is made, the Comms logs it in the Decision Log.

---

## MEETING RULES (ALL MEETINGS)

1. **Start on time.** Late starts waste everyone's time. If the Pilot is late, the meeting starts without them (they catch up).
2. **End on time.** If more time is needed, schedule a follow-up. Don't let meetings run over.
3. **Phones away.** Unless being used for the meeting (notes, screen share). No Slack, no email.
4. **One speaker at a time.** No side conversations. No interruptions.
5. **Stay on agenda.** Tangents are parked ("Let's take this offline") and noted for follow-up.
6. **Output every time.** Every meeting produces: notes (Comms), decisions (Decision Log), or action items (assigned with due dates). No output = the meeting shouldn't have happened.
7. **No meeting for what Slack can do.** If it's an update, a status check, or a simple question — use Slack. Meetings are for discussion, decisions, and alignment.
