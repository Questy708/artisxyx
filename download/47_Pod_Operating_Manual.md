# 47. POD OPERATING MANUAL

**xHansa Fellowship — Pod Operating Manual**
**For Xcitizens | Phases 1–4 (Months 1–24)**

---

## 1. WHAT A POD IS

A Pod is a 10-person operational unit. It is not a team, a group, or a committee. It is a deployment unit — the smallest functional entity in the Hansa League that can build, deploy, and scale a ProtoCo.

Your Pod is your family for 24 months. You will eat together, build together, fail together, and either pass the Gates together or hit the Kill Switch together. The Pod is the unit of success and the unit of failure.

---

## 2. POD COMPOSITION

### 2.1 The Ten Members

| Role | Archetype | Count | Function |
|------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Direction-setter | Pilot | 1 | Sets strategy, prioritizes, cuts scope, manages Gate trajectory |
| Constructor | Builder | 2–3 | Builds the Minimal Version, prototypes, technical artifact |
| Closer | Hustler | 1–2 | Secures Anchor Partner LOI, closes customer deals, negotiates |
| Stabilizer | Operator | 1–2 | Maintains operations, manages timelines, keeps the system running |
| Radar | Tracker | 1 | Spots risks, diagnoses problems, finds missing information |
| Voice/Memory | Comms | 1 | Authors the Playbook, manages partner communication, documents learnings |
| **Total** | | **10** | |

### 2.2 The Pod Leader
Your Pod Leader is NOT one of the 10. They are a senior Xcitizen (post-deployment) who supervises, mentors, and assesses the Pod. They:
- Run the weekly review
- Are your first point of escalation
- Advocate for the Pod to Strategic Command
- Assess Gate readiness (but do NOT determine Gate outcomes — that's Strategic Command)
- Do NOT build the ProtoCo (that's your job)

### 2.3 No Role Flexibility (Initially)
Your Archetype is your role. A Builder builds. A Pilot directs. A Tracker surveils. In the early phases (Q1–Q2), do not try to do each other's jobs. Specialize. Execute your Archetype.

In later phases (Q3–Q4), cross-Archetype capability develops naturally. But in Q1–Q2, stay in your lane. The Pod needs each Archetype at full strength, not everyone at 60% across all roles.

## 3. THE WEEKLY RHYTHM

### 3.1 Daily Standup (Async, by 08:00)
Every member posts in the Pod Slack channel:
```
**Standup — [Date]**
✅ Yesterday: [What you did]
→ Today: [What you're doing]
🚧 Blocked: [What's blocking you, or "Nothing"]
```
The Pod Leader reads these. If you're blocked, they help. If you're not posting, they'll notice.

### 3.2 Weekly Pod Review (Sync, 60 min)
Once a week (day set by the Pod Leader), the Pod meets for a structured review:

**Agenda:**
1. **Gate trajectory check (10 min):** Are we on track for the next Gate? What's ahead? What's behind?
2. **Individual reports (20 min):** Each member reports: what I did, what I'm doing, what I need.
3. **Blockers and risks (15 min):** What's stuck? What's at risk? (Tracker leads this section.)
4. **Commons contribution (10 min):** What are we submitting to the League Commons this week?
5. **Next week's plan (5 min):** The Pilot sets the priorities.

**Minutes:** The Comms documents and posts to the Pod channel in a thread.

### 3.3 Weekly Commons Contribution (Friday by 18:00)
Every Friday, the Pod submits a contribution to the League Commons:
- **What worked** (processes, solutions, breakthroughs)
- **What didn't** (failures, dead ends — especially valuable)
- **What we'd do differently**

The Comms drafts it. The Pilot reviews it. The Pod Leader reads it. It's posted to #commons-contributions on Slack and submitted to the Commons repository.

### 3.4 Monthly Strategic Review (90 min)
Once a month, the Pod meets with the Pod Leader for a deeper review:
- Gate trajectory (are we on track for the next Gate, which is [X] months away?)
- Budget burn (are we on budget? any unforeseen costs?)
- Risk register review (any new risks? any resolved?)
- Team health (Neural Link data review — is anyone burning out?)
- Pod dynamics (any conflicts? any communication breakdowns?)

## 4. DECISION-MAKING WITHIN THE POD

### 4.1 The Pilot Decides
The Pilot is the Pod's decision-maker. They set direction, prioritize, and make the calls. This is not a democracy. It is a deployment unit. Democratic decision-making is too slow for the pace the Gates demand.

### 4.2 Input Before Decision
The Pilot should seek input from all Archetypes before making a significant decision:
- **Builder:** "Can we build this? How long?"
- **Hustler:** "Can we sell this? To whom?"
- **Operator:** "Can we maintain this? At what cost?"
- **Tracker:** "What are the risks? What are we missing?"
- **Comms:** "How do we communicate this? To whom?"

But after input, the Pilot decides. The decision is final (unless overridden by Strategic Command or the Pod Leader, which is rare).

### 4.3 Escalation
If a member strongly disagrees with a Pilot's decision:
1. Raise it privately with the Pilot (not in front of the Pod)
2. If unresolved, raise it with the Pod Leader
3. The Pod Leader may mediate or escalate to Strategic Command
4. **In the field, execute the decision.** Disagree and commit. Do not undermine the Pilot in front of the Pod.

### 4.4 The Decision Log
Every significant decision is logged in the Decision Log (Document 50):
- Decision: [what was decided]
- Date: [when]
 decided by: [Pilot name]
- Rationale: [why]
- Input received: [from whom]
- Dissent: [any disagreement, and how it was resolved]
- Review date: [when to revisit, if applicable]

The Comms maintains the Decision Log. It's reviewed in the Weekly Review.

## 5. CONFLICT RESOLUTION

### 5.1 Within the Pod
Conflict is inevitable when 10 people operate under pressure for 24 months. The protocol:
1. **Address it directly.** The two parties talk privately. Calmly. Honestly. Per the Code of Conduct (radical honesty).
2. **If unresolved:** The Pilot mediates. (The Pilot is the Pod's authority.)
3. **If still unresolved:** The Pod Leader mediates.
4. **If it's a Conduct violation:** Go directly to the Conduct Officer.

### 5.2 Common Conflict Patterns

| Pattern | Cause | Resolution |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Pilot vs. Builder (speed vs. quality) | Pilot wants to ship; Builder wants to perfect | The Pilot decides (Gate deadlines are real). Builder executes. |
| Hustler vs. Operator (growth vs. stability) | Hustler wants to scale; Operator wants to stabilize | The Pilot decides based on Gate trajectory. |
| Tracker vs. Pod (risk vs. action) | Tracker flags risk; Pod wants to move | Tracker's risk is logged. Pilot decides whether to proceed. If proceed, the risk is documented and monitored. |
| Comms vs. everyone (documentation vs. action) | Comms wants to document; everyone wants to build | Comms documents in parallel. The Pod doesn't stop building to let Comms catch up. |

### 5.3 When Conflict Becomes Toxic
If conflict is corroding the Pod's ability to operate:
- The Pod Leader intervenes.
- In extreme cases, a member may be reassigned (rare — requires Strategic Command approval).
- The Pod must pass the Gate. If conflict causes a Gate failure, the Kill Switch applies to all 10.

## 6. THE POD AND THE GATES

### 6.1 The Gate is Pod-Level
Gates 1–4 are Pod-level. All 10 members pass or fail together. There is no individual Gate assessment (except Gate 0, which is individual).

### 6.2 Gate Preparation
Starting 4 weeks before a Gate:
- The Pod Leader runs a Gate-readiness review each week
- The Tracker identifies risks to Gate pass
- The Pilot adjusts priorities to address Gate gaps
- The Comms prepares the Gate submission documentation

### 6.3 Gate Submission
The Pod submits the Gate evidence (deployed Minimal Version, Anchor Partner LOI, revenue data, etc.) to Strategic Command via the venture tracker. The Pod Leader reviews before submission.

### 6.4 Gate Result
- **Pass:** The Pod advances to the next phase. Brief celebration. Then back to work.
- **Fail:** Kill Switch. The Pod is dissolved within 48 hours. See the Gate Criteria resource for the protocol.

### 6.5 No Individual Blame
If the Pod fails a Gate, it is a Pod failure — not an individual failure. The Kill Switch dissolves the Pod, but individuals are reassessed for reassignment (not automatically cycled out). Individual blame is a Conduct violation (Section 3 of the Code of Conduct — no politics).

## 7. THE POD AND THE LEAGUE COMMONS

### 7.1 Weekly Contribution
The Pod contributes to the Commons every Friday (Section 3.3). This is not optional. It is an obligation (Code of Conduct, Obligation 2: No Hoarding).

### 7.2 Active Commons Use
Before solving any problem, the Pod searches the Commons first. Another Pod may have already solved it. The Commons is the League's collective intelligence — use it.

### 7.3 Commons Requests
If the Pod needs something from the Commons that isn't there, post a request in #commons-contributions: "Has any Pod solved [problem] in [context]?" Other Pods respond. The answer becomes a Commons contribution.

### 7.4 The Playbook (Gate 3)
At Gate 3, the Pod delivers the Playbook — a codified operational manual for the ProtoCo. This becomes League Commons IP. It is the Pod's most significant Commons contribution. Start writing it in Month 1 (see Document 64, Playbook Template).

---

## 8. THE POD AND OTHER PODS

### 8.1 Pod Privacy
Pods do not share operational details with each other (each Pod's venture tracker data is Pod-private). This prevents contamination (one Pod's failure shouldn't demoralize others) and preserves the integrity of the Gate system.

### 8.2 Commons Collaboration
Pods collaborate through the Commons — not directly. If Pod A has a supply chain breakthrough, it goes into the Commons. Pod B finds it in the Commons. They don't need to talk to each other.

### 8.3 Field Community
Pods in the same Civilizational Field share a Field community on TownSquare and a `#field-[name]` Slack channel. This is for Field-level discussion (domain knowledge, regulatory updates, vendor recommendations) — not Pod-specific operational details.

### 8.4 Cross-Pod Mentoring
Xcitizens can mentor across Pods (an experienced Builder mentoring a new Pod's Builder). This is facilitated through TownSquare and the Archetype communities. It does NOT involve sharing Pod operational data.

---

## 9. THE POD LEADER RELATIONSHIP

### 9.1 What the Pod Leader Does
- Runs the Weekly Review
- Runs the Monthly Strategic Review
- Is available for ad-hoc consultation (Slack, in-person)
- Reviews Gate submissions before they go to Strategic Command
- Advocates for the Pod (resources, escalations, partner introductions)
- Assesses individual Xcitizen performance (input for Keeper/Cycled determination)
- Monitors Pod health (Neural Link data, dynamics, burnout)

### 9.2 What the Pod Leader Does NOT Do
- Does not build the ProtoCo (that's the Pod's job)
- Does not make operational decisions (the Pilot does)
- Does not determine Gate outcomes (Strategic Command does)
- Does not override the Kill Switch

### 9.3 How to Work with Your Pod Leader
- **Be honest.** Report failures immediately. Hiding problems from your Pod Leader is a Conduct violation.
- **Use them.** They're a resource. They've been through deployment. They know things.
- **Don't over-rely.** They're not your parent. They're your supervisor. Execute first; ask for help second.
- **Respect the chain.** Don't go to Strategic Command over your Pod Leader's head (unless it's a Conduct violation, in which case go to the Conduct Officer).

---

## 10. THE POD AT THE CLIFF (MONTH 24)

At Month 24, the Pod dissolves. The venture spins out (if Gate 4 passed) or is absorbed into the Commons (if Gate 4 failed). Outcomes for the 10 members:

- **Keepers (top 3% across the cohort):** 0–1 per Pod (statistically). Transition to permanent payroll in the spun-out entity.
- **The Cycled (97%):** 9–10 per Pod. Severed with performance equity (36-month vesting). XCitizen status retained.

The Pod that built together for 24 months scatters. This is not a failure. It is the design. The venture survives. The network survives. The Pod does not.

**What survives the Pod:**
- The venture (operated by local permanent operators)
- The Playbook (in the Commons, forever)
- The Commons contributions (weekly learnings, forever)
- The XCitizen relationships (the network, for life)
- The equity (vested over 36 months for the Cycled)
