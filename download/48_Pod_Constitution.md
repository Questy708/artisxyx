# 48. POD CONSTITUTION

**xHansa Fellowship — Pod Constitution**
**Pod [ID] | ProtoCo: [Name] | Field: [Field]**

---

## PREAMBLE

We, the 10 Xcitizens of Pod [ID], hereby adopt this Constitution as our operational covenant. We adopt it freely, understanding that the Pod is our deployment unit for 24 months — that we succeed or fail together, that the Gate is Pod-level, and that the Kill Switch dissolves us all.

This Constitution does not override the Fellowship Agreement, IP Agreement, NDA, or Code of Conduct. It governs how we operate within those agreements — the specific rules, norms, and commitments we make to each other.

---

## ARTICLE 1: THE POD'S PURPOSE

### 1.1 Mission
Our Pod's mission is to build, deploy, and scale [ProtoCo Name] — a commissioned venture in [Civilizational Field] — across the 24-month deployment, passing all four quarterly Gates and producing a spun-out venture operated by local permanent operators at Month 24.

### 1.2 Success Definition
Success is:
- Passing Gate 1 (Minimal Version + Anchor LOI) by Month 6
- Passing Gate 2 (Revenue + unit economics) by Month 12
- Passing Gate 3 (Replication + Playbook) by Month 18
- Passing Gate 4 (Local operator transition + 80%+ efficiency) by Month 24
- Delivering the Playbook to the League Commons
- Contributing weekly to the League Commons

Success is NOT:
- Individual recognition
- Keeper status (that's a byproduct, not a goal)
- Personal IP ownership (the Supremacy Clause governs)
- Employment after Month 24 (the cliff is the cliff)

### 2.2 Membership Changes
- Membership is frozen from Crucible Day 1 through Gate 0.
- After Gate 0, membership is frozen through the deployment (Months 1–24).
- The ONLY ways membership changes:
  1. **Attrition:** A member drops out (voluntary withdrawal or medical). The Pod runs with 9 until a waitlist replacement is sourced (within 1 week) or the Pod is dissolved (if no replacement and the Pod can't function with 9).
  2. **Kill Switch:** The Pod fails a Gate. All 10 are dissolved.
  3. **Reassignment (rare):** Strategic Command reassigns a member for Pod composition reasons (e.g., an Archetype mismatch discovered post-Crucible). Requires Strategic Command approval.

### 2.3 No Pod-Initiated Expulsions
The Pod cannot expel a member. If a member is not performing, the Pod Leader addresses it (with retraining, role adjustment, or escalation to Strategic Command). Individual expulsion is a Strategic Command decision, not a Pod decision.

## ARTICLE 3: GOVERNANCE

### 3.1 The Pilot is the Authority
The Pilot is the Pod's operational authority. They set direction, make decisions, and manage the Gate trajectory. This is not a democracy. It is a deployment unit.

### 3.2 Input Before Decision
The Pilot seeks input from all members before making significant decisions. But after input, the Pilot decides. The decision is final (unless overridden by Strategic Command or the Pod Leader).

### 3.3 Disagree and Commit
If a member disagrees with a Pilot's decision:
1. Raise it privately with the Pilot.
2. If unresolved, raise it with the Pod Leader.
3. **In the field, execute the decision.** Do not undermine the Pilot in front of the Pod or external parties.

### 3.4 The Pod Leader
The Pod Leader is the Pod's supervisor (not a member). They:
- Run the Weekly Review and Monthly Strategic Review
- Are the first point of escalation
- Advocate for the Pod to Strategic Command
- Do NOT make operational decisions (the Pilot does)
- Do NOT determine Gate outcomes (Strategic Command does)

## ARTICLE 4: COMMITMENTS TO EACH OTHER

### 4.1 Radical Honesty
We will tell each other the truth — about progress, failures, risks, and concerns — immediately and without spin. Hiding a failure from the Pod is worse than the failure itself.

### 4.2 Mutual Support
We will support each other's execution. If a member is struggling, we help. If a member is succeeding, we amplify. The Pod succeeds or fails together.

### 4.3 Respect for Roles
We will respect each other's Archetype and role. The Builder builds. The Pilot directs. The Hustler closes. We do not do each other's jobs (in Q1–Q2). We specialize and trust.

### 4.4 No Politics
We will not form factions, lobby the Pod Leader for favor, or undermine each other. The Gate System eliminates politics. We will not reintroduce them.

### 4.5 The Commons First
We will contribute to the League Commons weekly. When we solve a problem, we share it. When we fail, we share that too. Individual hoarding is a Conduct violation.

### 4.6 Respect the Kill Switch
If our Pod fails a Gate, we accept the Kill Switch without negotiation. We do not appeal, lobby, or fight. We cooperate with the 48-hour dissolution. We part with dignity.

## ARTICLE 5: OPERATIONAL NORMS

### 5.1 Daily Standup
Every member posts a daily standup in the Pod Slack channel by 08:00:
```
✅ Yesterday: [What you did]
→ Today: [What you're doing]
🚧 Blocked: [What's blocking you, or "Nothing"]
```

### 5.2 Weekly Review
The Pod meets once a week (day set by the Pod Leader) for a 60-minute structured review. Attendance is mandatory. Agenda: Gate trajectory, individual reports, blockers, Commons contribution, next week's plan.

### 5.3 Commons Contribution
The Pod submits a Commons contribution every Friday by 18:00. The Comms drafts it. The Pilot reviews it.

### 5.4 Decision Log
Every significant decision is logged in the Decision Log (Document 50). The Comms maintains it.

### 5.5 Meeting Discipline
- Meetings start on time. Late members are noted (pattern of lateness = risk flag).
- Meetings end on time. If more time is needed, schedule a follow-up.
- Phones away during meetings (unless being used for the meeting).
- One speaker at a time. No side conversations.

---

## ARTICLE 6: CONFLICT RESOLUTION

### 6.1 Direct First
Conflicts are addressed directly between the parties first. Calmly. Honestly. Privately.

### 6.2 Escalation
If direct resolution fails:
1. The Pilot mediates.
2. If unresolved, the Pod Leader mediates.
3. If it's a Conduct violation, the Conduct Officer adjudicates.

### 6.3 No Public Conflict
Pod conflicts are NOT discussed in cohort channels, with other Pods, or on TownSquare. They stay within the Pod (and the Pod Leader / Conduct Officer as needed).

### 6.4 No Retaliation
Raising a concern or conflict is protected. Retaliation against a member who raises a concern is a Conduct violation.

---

## ARTICLE 7: AMENDMENTS

### 7.1 Pod-Level Amendments
This Constitution can be amended by:
1. A proposal from any member
2. Discussion in a Weekly Review
3. A Pod vote (7/10 majority required)
4. Pod Leader approval

### 7.2 What Cannot Be Amended
- The Gate System (Strategic Command controls)
- The Kill Switch (Strategic Command controls)
- The Supremacy Clause (IP Agreement controls)
- The Code of Conduct (League controls)
- The cliff (Fellowship Agreement controls)

The Constitution governs Pod operations ONLY within the framework of the League's agreements. It cannot override them.

---

## ARTICLE 8: ADOPTION

### 8.1 Signing
All 10 members sign this Constitution on Crucible Day 1 (the first day the Pod meets). Signing is voluntary — but refusal to sign means refusal to deploy.

### 8.2 Review
The Constitution is reviewed at the Monthly Strategic Review. Amendments are proposed and voted on as needed.

### 8.3 Termination
This Constitution terminates when the Pod terminates — at the cliff (Month 24), via the Kill Switch, or via dissolution for other reasons. At termination, the Pod's obligations to each other end (but obligations to the League — confidentiality, IP, etc. — survive per the agreements).
