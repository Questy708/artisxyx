# 49. WEEKLY SPRINT GUIDE

**xHansa Fellowship — Pod Weekly Sprint Guide**
**For Xcitizens | Phases 1–4**

---

## PURPOSE

The Sprint is the Pod's weekly operational cycle. Every week, the Pod runs a sprint: plan on Monday, execute Tuesday–Friday, review on Friday, contribute to the Commons. This guide defines the sprint structure.

---

## THE SPRINT CYCLE

### Monday — Sprint Planning (30 min, with Pod Leader)

**Agenda:**
1. **Review last week's sprint** (10 min): Did we complete what we planned? What slipped? Why?
2. **Gate trajectory check** (5 min): How many weeks until the next Gate? Are we on track?
3. **Set this week's sprint goal** (10 min): What's the ONE thing we must accomplish this week to stay on Gate trajectory? (The Pilot sets this.)
4. **Break down the goal into tasks** (5 min): Each member takes specific tasks aligned with their Archetype.

**Output:** A sprint goal + task list, posted to the Pod Slack channel by end of Monday.

**Sprint goal format:**
```
**Sprint [Week X] Goal:** [One sentence — what must be true by Friday]
**Tasks:**
- [Pilot]: [task]
- [Builder 1]: [task]
- [Builder 2]: [task]
- [Hustler]: [task]
- [Operator]: [task]
- [Tracker]: [task]
- [Comms]: [task]
**Gate proximity:** [X] weeks to Gate [N]
```

### Tuesday–Thursday — Execution

- **Daily standup by 08:00** (async on Slack): yesterday / today / blocked
- **Execute your tasks.** Stay in your Archetype lane.
- **Blockers:** If you're blocked for more than 4 hours, flag it in the Pod channel. The Pilot reprioritizes.
- **Ad-hoc syncs:** Use Slack Huddles for quick discussions. Don't wait for the weekly review if something is urgent.

### Friday — Sprint Review + Commons Contribution (60 min)

**Agenda:**
1. **Sprint goal check** (10 min): Did we accomplish the sprint goal? If not, why? What slips to next week?
2. **Individual reports** (20 min): Each member reports on their tasks.
3. **Risk review** (10 min): The Tracker presents any new risks. Update the Risk Register.
4. **Commons contribution** (15 min): The Comms drafts the weekly Commons contribution. The Pod reviews and approves.
5. **Next week preview** (5 min): The Pilot previews next week's sprint goal.

**Output:**
- Sprint review notes (posted to Pod channel by Comms)
- Commons contribution (posted to #commons-contributions by 18:00)
- Updated Risk Register (in Notion)
- Updated Decision Log (if any decisions were made this week)

## SPRINT PLANNING PRINCIPLES

### 1. One Goal Per Sprint
The sprint has ONE primary goal. Not three. Not five. One. If you accomplish nothing else, accomplishing the sprint goal means the week was a success.

### 2. Gate-Aligned
Every sprint goal must trace to a Gate criterion. If the sprint goal doesn't move you toward a Gate, don't do it. (The exception: Commons contributions and mandatory operational tasks.)

### 3. Archetype-Aligned
Tasks are assigned by Archetype. The Builder builds. The Hustler closes. The Tracker surveils. Don't assign a Builder a Hustler task (unless the Hustler is overloaded and the Builder has capacity — but even then, it's a stretch assignment, not a permanent realignment).

### 4. Realistic Scope
Sprint goals are aggressive but achievable. If you consistently miss sprint goals, you're planning too aggressively — and you're risking a Gate failure. The Pilot adjusts.

### 5. Buffer for the Unexpected
Leave 20% of the sprint capacity as buffer. Something will go wrong. A vendor will delay. A customer will churn. A team member will get sick. The buffer absorbs it.

## SPRINT ANTI-PATTERNS

### Anti-Pattern 1: The Overloaded Sprint
**Symptom:** 15 tasks, no buffer, everyone stretched thin.
**Fix:** The Pilot cuts scope. One goal. Fewer tasks. 20% buffer.

### Anti-Pattern 2: The Vague Sprint
**Symptom:** Sprint goal is "make progress on the ProtoCo." No specific tasks.
**Fix:** The Pilot defines a measurable, specific goal. "Deploy Minimal Version to 5 users" is a goal. "Make progress" is not.

### Anti-Pattern 3: The Archetype Swap
**Symptom:** The Builder is doing Hustler tasks (closing deals) because the Hustler is "busy."
**Fix:** The Hustler prioritizes. If the Hustler is genuinely overloaded, the Pod Leader helps rebalance. Don't silently swap roles — it leads to resentment and poor execution.

### Anti-Pattern 4: The Skipped Review
**Symptom:** "We're too busy to do the Friday review."
**Fix:** Never skip the review. If you're too busy, the review is MORE important — it's where you catch the slippage before it becomes a Gate failure.

### Anti-Pattern 5: The No-Commons Week
**Symptom:** "We didn't learn anything worth contributing this week."
**Fix:** You always learned something. Even if it's "this approach didn't work." Failures are especially valuable Commons contributions. Submit something every week. No exceptions.
