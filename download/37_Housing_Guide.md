# 37. HOUSING GUIDE

**xHansa Fellowship — Housing Guide**

---

## 1. OVERVIEW

The League provides housing for all Xcitizens during the Crucible (8 weeks) and during field deployment (Phases 1–4). This guide covers what to expect, how to manage your housing, and what to do if there are issues.

---

## 2. CRUCIBLE HOUSING (WEEKS 1–8)

### 2.1 What's Provided
- **Type:** Shared housing (apartment or house) near the XEmbassy, OR dormitory-style housing at the XEmbassy itself (varies by location)
- **Distance:** Within 15 minutes' walk or 5 minutes' ride of the XEmbassy
- **Room:** Private bedroom (bed, desk, locker, lamp). Shared bathroom (1 per 2–4 Xcitizens).
- **Common areas:** Shared kitchen, living area, laundry
- **Utilities:** Wi-Fi, water, electricity, gas — all included
- **Linens:** Bed sheets, pillow, blanket provided (bring your own if you prefer)
- **Cleaning:** Common areas cleaned weekly by League-arranged service. You clean your own room.

### 2.2 Roommates
You may share housing with 1–7 other Xcitizens (from your cohort, potentially from other Pods). Roommate assignments are made by the Housing Coordinator based on:
- Same XEmbassy location
- Compatible Archetypes (avoid putting two "solo-operator-tendency" flags together)
- Gender preferences (if declared)

You will NOT share a room with a member of your own Pod (this prevents intra-Pod dynamics from bleeding into living spaces).

### 2.3 House Rules
1. **Quiet hours:** 22:00–05:30. No exceptions during the Crucible — you need sleep.
2. **Cleanliness:** Clean up after yourself in common areas. Your room is your responsibility.
3. **Guests:** No overnight guests during the Crucible. Daytime visitors must be XCitizens (no external guests without Hub Manager approval).
4. **Substances:** No illegal drugs. Alcohol in moderation, off-hours only. Intoxication is a Conduct violation.
5. **Respect:** Respect your housemates' space, sleep, and belongings.
6. **Security:** Lock doors when leaving. Don't let non-Xcitizens into the housing.
7. **Neural Link:** Wear it 24/7, including while sleeping (it tracks sleep patterns — this is data the medical team needs).

### 2.4 Meals
- **Breakfast (07:00) and dinner (18:00):** Provided at the XEmbassy commons. Eat there — it's also social time with the cohort.
- **Lunch (12:00):** Provided at the XEmbassy commons (30-minute break during the Crucible).
- **Snacks/late-night:** Your housing has a shared kitchen. Stock it with your own snacks (at your expense).
- **Dietary restrictions:** Communicate to the kitchen staff via the Hub Manager on Day 1.

### 2.5 Laundry
- Washing machine + dryer in the housing (or nearby laundromat)
- Detergent provided
- During the Crucible: do laundry on weekends (you won't have time during the week)
- During deployment: manage your own schedule

## 3. DEPLOYMENT HOUSING (PHASES 1–4)

### 3.1 Phase 1 (Q1: Months 1–6) — Pilot Site
After the Crucible, you may relocate to your ProtoCo's pilot deployment site. Housing:
- **Type:** Shared apartment or League-arranged housing near the deployment site
- **If the pilot site is the same city as the XEmbassy:** You may stay in your Crucible housing
- **If the pilot site is a different city/country:** The League arranges new housing
- **Duration:** Through Phase 1 (6 months)

### 3.2 Phase 2 (Q2: Months 7–12) — Revenue Site
Typically the same location as Phase 1 (you're monetizing the pilot). Housing remains the same unless the Pod relocates.

### 3.3 Phase 3 (Q3: Months 13–18) — Expansion
This is the most complex housing phase. You may travel to 3–5 geographies for replication:
- **Primary housing:** Remains at your main deployment site
- **Replication travel housing:** Hotels or short-term rentals arranged by Logistics (see Travel Guide, Doc 36)
- **Extended stays:** If you're in a replication site for 2+ weeks, the League arranges a short-term apartment

### 3.4 Phase 4 (Q4: Months 19–24) — Institutionalization
You transition out as local operators take over. Housing:
- **If you're a Keeper:** You may relocate to the spun-out entity's HQ (housing arranged by the entity)
- **If you're Cycled:** League housing continues until the cliff date (Month 24). After the cliff, you arrange your own departure (the League covers one return flight home).

### 3.5 Housing for Partners/Family
League housing is for Xcitizens only. If you have a partner or family:
- The League does NOT provide family housing
- You may arrange separate housing for your family (at your expense)
- Your stipend accounts for your needs only, not dependents (though the stipend amount is needs-based and may be higher if you have dependents — see Offer Letter)
- Dependents cannot stay in League housing

## 4. HOUSING MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 The Housing Coordinator
Each XEmbassy has a Housing Coordinator who:
- Assigns rooms
- Handles maintenance requests
- Manages check-in/check-out
- Resolves housing disputes

Contact: [Housing Coordinator name] — [phone/email] — or via #housing-[city] on Slack

### 4.2 Maintenance Issues
Report maintenance issues (plumbing, electrical, appliances, Wi-Fi) to the Housing Coordinator immediately. Do not attempt repairs yourself (liability). Emergency maintenance (water leak, electrical hazard, lock failure) is handled within 4 hours; non-emergency within 48 hours.

### 4.3 Check-Out
When you leave League housing (end of deployment, reassignment, or cycling out):
- Remove all personal belongings
- Clean your room (or a cleaning fee is deducted from your final stipend)
- Return keys / access badges to the Housing Coordinator
- Complete the check-out form (room condition, missing items, feedback)

See the Packing Guide (Document 38) for the full list. Housing-specific essentials:

## 6. HOUSING ISSUES & DISPUTES

### 6.1 Roommate Conflicts
If you have a conflict with a housemate:
1. **Try to resolve it directly** (calmly, honestly, per the Code of Conduct)
2. **If unresolved:** Escalate to the Housing Coordinator (they can mediate)
3. **If still unresolved:** Escalate to the Hub Manager (they can reassign rooms)
4. **If it's a Conduct violation** (harassment, theft, etc.): Go directly to the Conduct Officer

### 6.2 Housing Quality Issues
If your housing is substandard (unsafe, unsanitary, non-functional):
1. Document the issue (photos)
2. Report to the Housing Coordinator
3. If not resolved within 48 hours, escalate to the Hub Manager
4. The League's standard: housing must be safe, clean, functional, and within 15 min of the XEmbassy. If it's not, it gets fixed.

---

## 7. POST-CLIFF HOUSING

After Month 24:
- **Keepers:** Housing transitions to the spun-out entity's arrangement
- **Cycled:** League housing ends on the cliff date. You have 7 days to vacate (the League covers accommodation for those 7 days). After that, you arrange your own housing. The League covers one return flight to your home city.

**Plan ahead.** If you're likely to be Cycled (97% probability), start thinking about post-deployment housing around Month 20. The network (TownSquare, alumni) can help with leads.
