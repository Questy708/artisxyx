Thank you for your application to the xHansa Fellowship. We appreciate the time, honesty, and effort you put into the process.

I'm writing to let you know that we are **not offering you a deployment** in Cohort [N].

**Why we're writing this letter the way we are:**
We don't give individualized feedback — the volume of applications (we received [number] this cohort) makes it impossible. But we believe you deserve more than a form letter. So here are the common reasons for decline, and what we'd say to you directly.

**Common reasons for decline (in order of frequency):**

1. **Commitment terms not fully accepted.** The xHansa Fellowship requires unconditional acceptance of the 24-month cliff, the $500–$1,200 stipend (not salary), the Supremacy Clause (IP to the League Commons), and the binary Gate system. Hesitation on any of these — even subtle hesitation — is a signal. The program is designed as a commitment device; if the commitment isn't clean, it won't hold under the Crucible.

2. **Insufficient evidence of operating.** We select on evidence that you build, run, fix, deploy, or scale — not on credentials, university, or work history. If the application described plans, intentions, or aspirations more than tangible artifacts (things you've actually shipped), that's the gap.

3. **Typing Assessment did not surface a clear execution-first Archetype.** The assessment observes behavior under stress. If the behavior showed compliance conditioning (waiting for permission, deferring, asking for instruction) more than execution capability (deciding, acting, reporting), the Archetype won't be assignable.

4. **Self-awareness gaps.** The application's "honest part" (hardest thing done, what happens under cognitive exhaustion, a belief most disagree with) is weighted heavily. Performative answers, claiming to have no weaknesses, or blaming external factors for past failures are signals.

**What we'd say to you directly:**

If the terms scared you — that's okay. The program is not for everyone, and self-knowledge is a form of strength we respect. There are other paths to building for emerging markets (the xCelero Accelerator, Inception Studios, or building on your own).

If you believe the assessment missed who you are — apply again. Cohort [N+1] applications open [March 1, 2026]. We re-review every application fresh. The Typing Assessment is not a permanent label; behavior changes, and we're willing to look again.

If you're angry right now — channel it. Build something. Ship it. Show us what you operate. The strongest applications we've ever seen came from people who were declined once, went and built something real, and came back with evidence. We notice builders. We notice them especially when they build after being told no.

We read your application. We did not skim it. The fact that you applied — that you read the terms, understood the cliff, and still raised your hand — means something. The world needs more people willing to commit to building infrastructure for the Global South, regardless of whether they do it through xHansa.
