# xHANSA FELLOWSHIP — SELECTION RUBRIC & TYPING ASSESSMENT PROTOCOL

**INTERNAL — STRATEGIC COMMAND USE**

---

## 1. APPLICATION REVIEW RUBRIC

Each application is scored on four dimensions. The total score determines whether the applicant advances to the Typing Assessment. We do not score on credentials, university, or work history.

### Scoring Scale
Each dimension: 0–5 points. Total: 0–20. Advancement threshold: 12+.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | No evidence / does not meet |
| 1 | Minimal evidence |
| 2 | Some evidence, underdeveloped |
| 3 | Clear evidence, solid |
| 4 | Strong evidence, compelling |
| 5 | Exceptional — this is the person |

### Dimension 1: Evidence of Operating (Section 2 of Application)
*What have they built, run, fixed, deployed, or scaled?*

| Criterion | What we look for |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Built something real | A business, product, system, machine, community, supply chain — not a plan, not a slide deck |
| Operated under constraint | Evidence of resource constraint (informal economy, self-taught, bootstrapped) |
| Followed through | Completion > ideation. Did they ship it? Did it run? |
| Learned from it | Reflection on what worked, what didn't, what they'd do differently |

**Red flags:** Vague descriptions, "I helped" (not "I built"), résumé-padding, no tangible artifact.

### Dimension 2: Acceptance of Commitment Terms (Section 3 of Application)
*Do they understand and accept the terms?*

| Criterion | What we look for |
|-----------|-----------------|
| 24-month commitment | Unambiguous acceptance — not "I have questions" |
| Stipend terms | Accepts $500–$1,200, no salary, no benefits |
| Supremacy Clause | Accepts IP to League Commons — not reluctantly |
| Gate System + Kill Switch | Accepts binary outcomes, no appeals |
| The Cliff (3%/97%) | Accepts that 97% are cycled out — and that this is by design |
| The Crucible | Prepared for psychological intensity |

**Red flags:** Hesitation, "can we negotiate," conditional acceptance, treating the terms as a starting point.

### Dimension 3: Field Alignment + Deployable Thinking (Section 4 of Application)
*Which field calls to them, and can they think deployably?*

| Criterion | What we look for |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Field preference | 1–3 fields chosen with reasoning (not random) |
| Why those fields | Reasoning shows domain understanding, not trend-chasing |
| Deployable concept | If deployed tomorrow, what would they build? Is it specific, feasible, grounded? |
| Systems thinking | Understands the field as infrastructure, not a single product |

**Red flags:** "AI" or "blockchain" as an answer, no specificity, vision-statement language, no awareness of the actual bottleneck.

### Dimension 4: Self-Awareness Under Pressure (Sections 5–6 of Application)
*Do they know themselves — especially under stress?*

| Criterion | What we look for |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Archetype self-assessment | Honest, not aspirational (people who claim to be "Pilot" are often not) |
| Weakest archetype | Self-aware about gaps (strong signal) |
| Hardest thing done | Genuine difficulty, not humblebrag |
| Under cognitive exhaustion | Honest about how they degrade (the Crucible will find out anyway) |
| Disagreement belief | Independent thinking, not contrarian-for-its-own-sake |
| If rejected | Shows resilience and continued intent to build |

**Red flags:** Claiming to be good at everything, no weaknesses, performative answers, "I work too hard."

## 2. CIVILIZATIONAL FIELD TYPING ASSESSMENT — PROTOCOL

**Purpose:** To observe behavior under stress and assign each candidate a Deployment Classification (field) and an Archetype. This is NOT an interview. It is a behavioral observation.

**Duration:** 4–6 hours, conducted in-person at an XEmbassy or a designated assessment site.

**Format:** Candidates rotate through 5 scenario stations. Each station is timed, high-pressure, and designed to surface a specific Archetype trait. Assessors observe and score — they do not interview.

### The Five Stations

#### Station 1: The Wreckage (30 min)
*A simulated operational failure. The candidate is given a "running" system (a physical or digital setup) that is broken. They must diagnose and fix it under time pressure, with incomplete information, while an assessor interrupts with "urgent" updates.*

**Archetype signals:**
- **Pilot**: Steps back, sees the system, prioritizes the fix sequence
- **Builder**: Dives in, starts fixing, gets hands dirty
- **Operator**: Keeps the system running while others fix it
- **Tracker**: Identifies the root cause before anyone else
- **Hustler**: Manages the interruptions, keeps stakeholders calm
- **Comms**: Translates the problem to the "stakeholder" (assessor) clearly

#### Station 2: The Deal (30 min)
*A simulated negotiation. The candidate must secure a resource (an LOI, a partnership, a permit) from a role-player who is evasive, demanding, and time-constrained.*

**Archetype signals:**
- **Hustler**: Closes the deal
- **Pilot**: Reframes the negotiation to change the terms
- **Comms**: Makes the complex ask legible
- **Operator**: Manages the follow-up, the paperwork, the timeline
- **Tracker**: Identifies the role-player's real constraint
- **Builder**: Offers to build a prototype to seal it

#### Station 3: The Crowd (30 min)
*A group scenario. 6–8 candidates are given a shared problem with no designated leader. They must produce a deployable plan in 30 minutes. Assessors observe who leads, who stabilizes, who tracks, who builds, who closes, who translates.*

**Archetype signals:**
- **Pilot**: Sets direction (or emerges as leader)
- **Builder**: Starts constructing the plan/solution
- **Hustler**: Drives consensus, closes disagreements
- **Operator**: Keeps the group on time, on task
- **Tracker**: Spots the flaw in the plan
- **Comms**: Synthesizes and presents

#### Station 4: The Wall (45 min)
*Sustained cognitive load. The candidate is given a series of complex, ambiguous problems — one after another, with no break, for 45 minutes. Each problem is solvable but hard. The pace is relentless. This is a micro-Crucible.*

**Archetype signals (observed as the candidate fatigues):**
- Who keeps executing when spent? (**Operator**)
- Who starts cutting scope to ship? (**Pilot**)
- Who keeps building despite fatigue? (**Builder**)
- Who finds the shortcut? (**Tracker**)
- Who reframes the problem to make it solvable? (**Hustler**)
- Who documents so others can continue? (**Comms**)

#### Station 5: The Debrief (15 min)
*A one-on-one conversation. The assessor asks: "What just happened? What did you do? What should you have done differently?" This tests self-awareness — the ability to observe and adjust one's own behavior.*

**Archetype signals:**
- Accurate self-assessment (strong signal for all Archetypes)
- Blaming the scenario/tools/others (red flag)
- Ability to articulate what they'd change (growth signal)

## 3. ARCHETYPE ASSIGNMENT SCORING

Each assessor scores the candidate on each Archetype (0–5) at each station. The scores are aggregated.

### Assignment Rule
The candidate's **primary Archetype** = the highest aggregate score. The candidate's **secondary Archetype** = the second-highest (used for Pod composition balancing).

### Example Scoring Matrix (Candidate X)

| Station | Pilot | Builder | Hustler | Operator | Tracker | Comms |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| 1. Wreckage | 2 | 5 | 1 | 3 | 4 | 1 |
| 2. Deal | 3 | 2 | 5 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| 3. Crowd | 4 | 3 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 3 |
| 4. Wall | 3 | 4 | 2 | 5 | 3 | 2 |
| 5. Debrief | 4 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 4 |
| **Total** | **16** | **17** | **15** | **16** | **15** | **14** |

**Result:** Primary = Builder (17). Secondary = Pilot/Operator (16, tie — break by Wall score: Operator 5 > Pilot 3, so Secondary = Operator).

**Deployment Classification:** Builder-Operator. This candidate will be placed in a Pod needing a Builder with Operator depth.

## 4. DEPLOYMENT CLASSIFICATION (FIELD ASSIGNMENT)

After Archetype assignment, candidates are classified into one of the Nine Civilizational Fields based on:
1. Application preference (Section 4)
2. Typing Assessment domain reasoning (Station 5 debrief)
3. Commissioning needs (which fields have open ProtoCos this cohort)

Field assignment is needs-weighted: if Water Systems has 15 open ProtoCos and Energy Systems has 8, more candidates will be classified into Water Systems — regardless of preference. Preference is a tiebreaker, not a guarantee.

---

## 5. DECISION THRESHOLDS

| Outcome | Criteria |
|---------|----------|
| **Deploy** | Application score 12+ AND Typing Assessment shows clear primary Archetype (3+ point lead) AND field assignable |
| **Waitlist** | Application score 12+ BUT Archetype scores too flat (no clear primary) — hold for Pod composition balancing |
| **Decline** | Application score <12 OR commitment terms not accepted OR Typing Assessment reveals red flags (blame, rigidity, inability to operate under stress) |

**Target acceptance rate:** ~10% of applicants (to fill 1,000 seats from ~10,000 applications).

---

## 6. ASSESSOR TRAINING

Assessors must be XCitizens who have completed at least one full deployment cycle. Training:
- 2-day calibration workshop (score the same candidates, align on signals)
- Shadow 2 assessment days before leading a station
- Recalibration every 6 months

**Assessor bias mitigation:**
- Each candidate is scored by 3+ assessors across stations
- Scores are aggregated, not averaged by consensus (no groupthink)
- Assessor names are stripped from final scoring sheets
- Quarterly audit: do certain assessors consistently score higher/lower? Recalibrate.
