**xHansa Fellowship — Notion Workspace Guide**

## 1. WHAT NOTION IS FOR

Notion is the League's documentation workspace. Your Pod uses it to manage every aspect of the ProtoCo — from the initial dossier to the final Playbook. It is the single source of truth for your venture's documentation.

**What goes in Notion:**
- ProtoCo Dossier (Doc 55)
- Venture Canvas (Doc 56)
- Customer Discovery notes (Doc 57)
- Pilot Tracker (Doc 58)
- Experiment Log (Doc 59)
- Risk Register (Doc 60)
- Budget Tracker (Doc 61)
- KPI Dashboard (Doc 62)
- Decision Log (Doc 50)
- Meeting notes
- The Playbook (Doc 64 — Gate 3 deliverable)

**What does NOT go in Notion:**
- Confidential Strategic Communications (use Signal or in-person)
- Other Xcitizens' personal/medical data
- Anything you wouldn't want the Conduct Officer to read (Notion is League-accessible)

## 5. DAILY NOTION WORKFLOW

### Morning (before 08:00)
- Check the KPI Dashboard for yesterday's metrics
- Review the Risk Register for any new red flags
- Post your Slack standup (Section 4.3 of Slack Guide) based on Notion status

### During the Day
- Log customer discovery notes as interviews happen (don't batch — write immediately)
- Add experiment results to the Experiment Log as they come in
- Update the Pilot Tracker when deployment milestones occur
- Log decisions in the Decision Log (with rationale)

### Friday (by 18:00)
- Update the KPI Dashboard with the week's data
- Submit the weekly Commons contribution (summarize the week's learnings from Notion → post to Slack #commons-contributions + Commons repository)
- Review the Risk Register — any new risks? Any resolved?

## 6. THE PLAYBOOK (GATE 3 DELIVERABLE)

The Playbook is the most important document you'll produce. It's the codified operational manual that any future operator can execute without your Pod. It's a Gate 3 requirement (Month 18).

**Start the Playbook in Month 1.** Do not wait until Month 17. Add to it weekly. By Month 18, it should be 50+ pages and cover:
1. Setup (how to stand up the ProtoCo in a new geography)
2. Operations (daily/weekly/monthly procedures)
3. Maintenance (troubleshooting, repairs, monitoring)
4. Scaling (how to add a new site)
5. Local Adaptation (how to adapt to different regulatory/supply chain contexts)
6. Vendor Management (how to source, negotiate, manage)
7. Customer Management (acquisition, support, retention)
8. Financial Management (pricing, collections, reporting)
9. Risk Management (known risks and mitigations)
10. Appendices (templates, checklists, contact lists)

**Test:** At Gate 3, an XCitizen from another Pod must follow your Playbook for 1 day without your assistance and successfully operate the ProtoCo. If they can't, the Playbook fails.

## 7. NOTION BEST PRACTICES

### 7.1 Keep It Current
Notion is only useful if it's current. Stale data is worse than no data. If you haven't updated a page in 2 weeks, either update it or archive it.

### 7.2 Use Properties
Notion databases (tables with properties) are powerful. Use them for:
- Customer Discovery (each interview = a row with properties: date, name, geography, key insight)
- Experiments (each experiment = a row with hypothesis, result, learning)
- Risks (each risk = a row with severity, owner, status, mitigation)

### 7.3 Link Pages
Connect related pages. If a customer discovery note reveals a risk, link to the Risk Register entry. If an experiment informs the Playbook, link to the relevant section. Notion's strength is interconnectedness.

### 7.4 Keep It Clean
- Delete outdated pages (or archive them — don't leave them cluttering the workspace)
- Use consistent naming: `[Date] — [Topic]` for meetings, `[Number] — [Title]` for experiments
- One page per topic. Don't create "Misc" or "Notes" catch-all pages.

### 7.5 Comment and Discuss
Use Notion comments for async discussion on specific pages. Don't duplicate the discussion in Slack — keep it where the context is.

## 8. ACCESS & PRIVACY

- **Your Pod workspace:** Visible to your Pod (10) + Pod Leader + Strategic Command
- **Other Pods' workspaces:** Not visible (by design)
- **Shared templates:** Visible to all Pods (read-only)
- **The Commons:** Not part of Notion — use the Commons repository for shared IP

**Do not put in Notion:**
- Other Xcitizens' personal data
- Strategic Command deliberations
- Anything marked "Confidential — Signal only"

---

## 9. INTEGRATION WITH SLACK

Notion integrates with Slack:
- Get a notification in your Pod channel when a page is updated
- Get a notification when someone comments on a page you own
- Use `/notion` in Slack to search and share Notion pages

Configure this in Slack: Settings → Integrations → Notion.

## 10. TROUBLESHOOTING

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't access the workspace | Contact IT via #support on Slack |
| Template missing | Contact your Pod Leader — they can add League templates |
| Page accidentally deleted | Notion has a 30-day trash — recover from the sidebar → Trash |
| Slow performance | Large pages with many blocks can lag. Split into sub-pages if a page exceeds 100 blocks. |
| Can't find something | Use Notion search (Cmd/Ctrl+P). Check the workspace sidebar structure. |
