# 38. PACKING GUIDE

**xHansa Fellowship — Packing Guide**
**What to bring for 24 months of deployment**

---

## 1. THE PHILOSOPHY

Pack light. You're not moving to a new life — you're deploying to build one. Everything you need to survive is provided (housing, workspace, meals, equipment). Everything you bring should serve the deployment, your health, or your sanity.

**The rule:** If it doesn't help you build, rest, or stay healthy, leave it behind.

---

## 2. ESSENTIALS (DO NOT ARRIVE WITHOUT THESE)

### 2.1 Documents (Carry-On, Never Checked)
- [ ] **Passport** (valid 6+ months beyond deployment end date)
- [ ] **Visa** (if required — printed copy)
- [ ] **Acceptance letter** (printed — for immigration if questioned)
- [ ] **Travel insurance card** (League-provided)
- [ ] **Vaccination certificate** (yellow fever card if required by deployment country)
- [ ] **Medical information card** (allergies, conditions, medications, blood type — printed)
- [ ] **Emergency contacts** (Hub Manager phone, family contact — printed, offline)
- [ ] **Flight itinerary** (printed, in case of phone failure)

### 2.2 Money
- [ ] **Debit/credit card** (international, no foreign transaction fees if possible)
- [ ] **Cash** ($100–200 USD in the local currency of your deployment city, for immediate needs)
- [ ] **Backup card** (in case your primary is lost/stolen — keep separate from primary)

### 2.3 Technology
- [ ] **Laptop** + charger (see Technology Setup Guide, Doc 33, for specs)
- [ ] **Phone** + charger (unlocked, international)
- [ ] **Power adapter** (if deployment country uses different plugs — check before you fly)
- [ ] **Power bank** (10,000mAh+ — for field deployments where power is unreliable)
- [ ] **USB-C cable** (or your laptop/phone's charge cable — 2 cables, 1 is a backup)
- [ ] **Earbuds/headphones** (for calls, focus, and sanity)
- [ ] **USB drive** (32GB+ — for offline file transfer in the field)

### 2.4 Medical
- [ ] **Personal medications** (30-day supply — the medical team can refill locally)
- [ ] **Glasses/contacts** (if you wear them — bring a backup pair of glasses)
- [ ] **Allergy medication** (if applicable)
- [ ] **Basic first aid kit** (bandages, antiseptic, pain relievers — the XEmbassy has a full kit, but carry a small personal one for field travel)

---

## 3. CLOTHING

### 3.1 The Crucible (Weeks 1–8)
During the Crucible, you'll be on your feet, in workshops, under cognitive exhaustion. Pack for function, not fashion.

- [ ] **5–7 t-shirts** (comfortable, durable, you'll sweat in them)
- [ ] **2–3 long-sleeve shirts** (for layering in air-conditioned spaces)
- [ ] **2 pairs of durable pants** (jeans or work pants — not dress pants)
- [ ] **1 pair of shorts** (for off-hours)
- [ ] **5–7 pairs of underwear** (moisture-wicking if possible)
- [ ] **5–7 pairs of socks** (durable, not dress socks)
- [ ] **1 pair of comfortable, closed-toe shoes** (you'll be on your feet 12+ hours/day)
- [ ] **1 pair of sandals/flip-flops** (for housing, showers)
- [ ] **1 lightweight jacket** (for early mornings, air-conditioned spaces)
- [ ] **1 rain jacket** (if deploying to a rainy climate — check the season)

### 3.2 Deployment (Phases 1–4)
- [ ] **2–3 "professional" outfits** (for Anchor Partner meetings, customer meetings — a button-down or blouse, nice pants/skirt, closed shoes)
- [ ] **1 set of field clothes** (durable, stain-tolerant — you may be in workshops, construction sites, farms, clinics depending on your Field)
- [ ] **1 formal outfit** (for Demo Day, Gate reviews, potential press — a suit/dress/nice outfit)
- [ ] **Warm layers** (if deploying to a climate with cold seasons — check)
- [ ] **Sun hat** (if deploying to a sunny climate)
- [ ] **Work boots** (if your Field involves hardware, construction, agriculture — check with your Pod Leader)

### 3.3 General
- [ ] **Sleepwear** (comfortable — Crucible sleep is precious)
- [ ] **Swimwear** (optional — some XEmbassies have access to pools/beaches)
- [ ] **Gym clothes** (if you exercise — 2 outfits, wash regularly)

**Laundry note:** You'll do laundry weekly. Don't overpack — 7 days of clothes is enough. The League provides laundry facilities.

---

## 4. PERSONAL ITEMS

### 4.1 For Your Sanity
- [ ] **Notebook + 2 pens** (analog — you'll want it for the Reflection Journal, Doc 43, and for Crucible debriefs)
- [ ] **A book** (non-fiction or fiction — for the rare downtime. You won't have much, but when you do, don't look at a screen)
- [ ] **Photos of family/loved ones** (2–3 printed — for your room. The deployment is long and you'll want the reminder)
- [ ] **A comfort item** (whatever grounds you — a coffee mug, a hoodie, a music playlist downloaded offline)

### 4.2 For Your Health
- [ ] **Water bottle** (1L, durable — you'll carry it everywhere)
- [ ] **Sunscreen** (if deploying to a sunny climate)
- [ ] **Insect repellent** (if deploying to a tropical climate)
- [ ] **Multivitamins** (optional — the Commons meals are nutritious, but a supplement doesn't hurt)
- [ ] **Melatonin** (optional — for sleep regulation during travel and The Wall)

### 4.3 For The Field
- [ ] **Daypack/backpack** (20–30L, for carrying laptop, water, notebook to field sites)
- [ ] **Headlamp** (for field deployments in areas with unreliable power)
- [ ] **Multi-tool** (Leatherman or similar — invaluable for hardware/build work; check airline rules for carry-on vs. checked)

---

## 5. WHAT NOT TO BRING

### 5.1 Leave Behind
- ❌ **Your ego** (the Crucible will strip it anyway)
- ❌ **Your expectations** (they'll be wrong)
- ❌ **A "plan" for how to win** (the program is un-winnable in the traditional sense)
- ❌ **More than 2 bags** (you'll be moving between sites; heavy bags slow you down)
- ❌ **Valuable jewelry or watches** (they'll get lost or damaged in the field)
- ❌ **Fragile items** (deployment is rough on belongings)
- ❌ **Books beyond 1–2** (they're heavy and you won't have time to read much; the Commons has a library)
- ❌ **Gaming consoles / large electronics** (no space, no time)
- ❌ **Your entire wardrobe** (7 days of clothes is enough; you're not at a fashion show)

### 5.2 Prohibited
- ❌ **Illegal drugs** (zero tolerance — Conduct violation, immediate cycling out)
- ❌ **Weapons** (firearms, large knives — multi-tools are fine, weapons are not)
- ❌ **Pornography or offensive materials** (the League is a professional environment)
- ❌ **Personal business materials** (you can't run a side business during deployment — Section 7.2 of the Fellowship Agreement)

## 7. CLIMATE-SPECIFIC ADDITIONS

Check your deployment city's climate and add:

### Tropical (e.g., Lagos, Accra, Dar es Salaam)
- [ ] Extra moisture-wicking clothing
- [ ] Rain jacket (heavy)
- [ ] Insect repellent (DEET-based)
- [ ] Antimalarial medication (consult the medical team)
- [ ] Light, breathable fabrics

### Arid (e.g., Niamey, Bamako, Cairo)
- [ ] Sun hat (wide-brimmed)
- [ ] Sunglasses (UV-protective)
- [ ] Lip balm + moisturizer (dry climate)
- [ ] Dust scarf (for harmattan / dust storms)
- [ ] Extra water bottle

### Highland (e.g., Nairobi, Addis Ababa, Kigali)
- [ ] Warm layers (nights can be cold even in tropical countries)
- [ ] Light jacket (for evenings)
- [ ] Sunscreen (high-altitude UV is strong)

### Cold Season (check if your deployment includes winter months)
- [ ] Insulated jacket
- [ ] Warm base layers
- [ ] Gloves + beanie
- [ ] Warm socks
