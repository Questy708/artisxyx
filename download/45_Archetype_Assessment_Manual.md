# 45. ARCHETYPE ASSESSMENT MANUAL

**xHansa Fellowship — Archetype Assessment Manual**
**INTERNAL — CRUCIBLE FACILITATORS + STRATEGIC COMMAND**

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This manual defines the six Xcitizen Archetypes, the behavioral signals that identify each, the scoring methodology, and the rules for assignment. It is the authoritative reference for all Archetype assessments — during pre-deployment Typing, during the Crucible, and for Pod Formation.

---

## 2. THE SIX ARCHETYPES

### 2.1 The Pilot
**Core function:** Sets direction.

The Pilot sees the system before anyone else. They identify the path, set the priority sequence, and cut scope to ship. They emerge as leaders — not because they seek authority, but because their directional clarity makes them the natural reference point.

**Behavioral signals:**
- Steps back from the immediate problem to see the system
- Prioritizes: identifies what matters most and sequences it
- Cuts scope: recognizes when "good enough" is better than "perfect"
- Reframes: when the group is stuck, changes the question
- Decides under uncertainty: acts without full information, then adjusts

**Under stress (The Wall):** The Pilot starts cutting scope aggressively. They may appear to "give up" on perfection — but they're actually prioritizing ship-ability. They maintain decision-making when everything else degrades.

**Risk pattern:** The "solo-Pilot" — a Pilot who doesn't listen to the Pod and decides unilaterally. This is a risk flag for Pod formation (don't pair two solo-Pilots).

**Deployment role:** Sets the ProtoCo's direction. Makes the "what do we build next" calls. Manages the Gate trajectory. The Pilot is the Pod's strategic compass.

---

### 2.2 The Builder
**Core function:** Constructs.

The Builder turns ideas into working things. They dive in, get their hands dirty, and prototype before planning. They are the majority of every Pod (2–3 per Pod) because building is the primary activity of a venture.

**Behavioral signals:**
- Dives in immediately — starts constructing before the plan is finished
- Gets hands dirty (literal or metaphorical)
- Prototypes: makes a rough version, tests it, iterates
- Maintains construction momentum under fatigue
- Focuses on the artifact, not the presentation

**Under stress (The Wall):** The Builder keeps building. They may lose sight of the bigger picture (the Pilot's job to maintain), but they don't stop producing. The last thing to go is their construction instinct.

**Risk pattern:** The "tactical Builder" — a Builder who builds without diagnosing first. They ship fast but may ship the wrong thing. Mitigated by pairing with a strong Tracker.

**Deployment role:** Builds the Minimal Version. Constructs the prototypes. Maintains and upgrades the technical artifact throughout deployment. The Builder is the Pod's engine.

---

### 2.3 The Hustler
**Core function:** Closes.

The Hustler secures the deal, the partner, the customer. They reframe problems to make them solvable. They manage stakeholders and keep relationships functional under pressure.

**Behavioral signals:**
- Closes: gets to "yes" when others get to "maybe"
- Reframes: when the group is stuck, changes the angle of approach
- Manages stakeholders: keeps the assessor/partner/customer calm and engaged
- Secures resources: gets things from the environment (information, materials, access)
- Reads people: identifies what the other party actually wants

**Under stress (The Wall):** The Hustler maintains relationships. They keep the Pod cohesive when fatigue makes everyone irritable. They reframe problems that seem impossible when you're exhausted.

**Risk pattern:** The "over-promiser" — a Hustler who offers things they don't have authority to offer. This is a serious deployment risk (it creates liability for the League). Flagged in the Typing Assessment.

**Deployment role:** Secures the Anchor Partner LOI (Gate 1). Closes customer deals (Gate 2). Negotiates replication partnerships (Gate 3). The Hustler is the Pod's closer.

---

### 2.4 The Operator
**Core function:** Maintains.

The Operator keeps the system running when everyone else is spent. They manage timelines, follow-ups, logistics, and the hundred small things that keep a venture from collapsing. They are the stabilizer.

**Behavioral signals:**
- Keeps the system running while others fix it
- Manages timelines: tracks deadlines, follow-ups, commitments
- Maintains composure under pressure (the calmest person in the room)
- Keeps executing when others are spent
- Patches operational gaps in real-time

**Under stress (The Wall):** The Operator is the last to break. They maintain execution when everyone else is degrading. They hold the Pod together through sheer operational discipline. The last thing to go is their execution consistency.

**Risk pattern:** The "invisible Operator" — an Operator whose contributions are so background that they're under-assessed. Facilitators must actively look for Operator signals; they don't announce themselves.

**Deployment role:** Maintains the ProtoCo's operations throughout deployment. Manages the supply chain, the customer support, the logistics. The Operator is the Pod's backbone.

---

### 2.5 The Tracker
**Core function:** Surveils.

The Tracker identifies the root cause, spots the risk, and finds the missing information. They see the flaw in the plan before it materializes. They are the Pod's early warning system.

**Behavioral signals:**
- Identifies the root cause before others (diagnoses before acting)
- Spots risks and flaws in the plan
- Finds the shortcut or the missing information
- Flags problems before they materialize
- Surveils: monitors data, patterns, and the environment for signals

**Under stress (The Wall):** The Tracker maintains observation. They may become quieter under fatigue (internal processing), but their risk-spotting doesn't degrade. They find the shortcut that saves the Pod.

**Risk pattern:** The "paralysis Tracker" — a Tracker who identifies risks but can't act on them. They flag problems but don't help solve them. Mitigated by pairing with a strong Builder or Pilot.

**Deployment role:** Spots risks before they hit (Gate failures, supply chain breaks, market shifts). Identifies the root cause when the ProtoCo underperforms. The Tracker is the Pod's radar.

---

### 2.6 The Comms
**Core function:** Translates.

The Comms makes the complex legible. They synthesize, document, and present. They translate between the Pod and stakeholders (partners, customers, Strategic Command). They ensure that knowledge is captured, not lost.

**Behavioral signals:**
- Translates the problem to stakeholders clearly
- Synthesizes: takes a complex situation and makes it understandable
- Documents: writes it down so others can continue
- Presents: makes the Pod's work legible to outsiders
- Makes the complex legible (not the same as "simplifying" — it's clarifying)

**Under stress (The Wall):** The Comms maintains documentation. When everyone else is too tired to write, the Comms keeps the record. This is invaluable — the Pod's learnings survive because the Comms captures them.

**Risk pattern:** The "passive Comms" — a Comms who documents but doesn't advocate. They record the meeting but don't shape it. A strong Comms doesn't just translate; they ensure the right message reaches the right person.

**Deployment role:** Authors the Playbook (Gate 3 deliverable). Manages partner communication. Documents the Pod's learnings for the League Commons. The Comms is the Pod's voice and memory.

## 3. SCORING METHODOLOGY

### 3.1 The 0–5 Scale
Each facilitator scores each Xcitizen on each Archetype, per scenario block, on a 0–5 scale:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 0 | No evidence of this Archetype in this scenario |
| 1 | Minimal — one faint signal |
| 2 | Some — a few signals, not dominant |
| 3 | Clear — multiple signals, a notable behavior |
| 4 | Strong — a primary behavior in this scenario |
| 5 | Dominant — this is how the Xcitizen operated in this scenario |

### 3.2 Aggregation
- Scores are aggregated across all scenarios and all facilitators.
- **Primary Archetype** = the highest aggregate score. Must have a 3+ point lead over the secondary to be "clear."
- **Secondary Archetype** = the second-highest aggregate.
- If the top two scores are within 2 points (a "flat profile"), the Xcitizen is **waitlisted** (not deployed) — the Pod Formation algorithm requires a clear primary.

### 3.3 Example Scoring Matrix

| Station/Scenario | Pilot | Builder | Hustler | Operator | Tracker | Comms |
|-----------------|-------|---------|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| Mission Card 1: Wreckage | 2 | 5 | 1 | 3 | 4 | 1 |
| Mission Card 2: Deal | 3 | 2 | 5 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| Mission Card 3: Crowd | 4 | 3 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 3 |
| The Wall (aggregate) | 3 | 4 | 2 | 5 | 3 | 2 |
| Mission Card 5: Debrief | 4 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 4 |
| **Total** | **16** | **17** | **15** | **16** | **15** | **14** |

**Result:** Primary = Builder (17). Secondary = Operator (16, tie with Pilot — break by Wall score: Operator 5 > Pilot 3, so Secondary = Operator). Deployment Classification: Builder-Operator.

## 4. ASSIGNMENT RULES

### 4.1 Primary Archetype
- Must have a 3+ point lead over the secondary.
- If the lead is < 3 points, the profile is "flat" → waitlist.

### 4.2 Secondary Archetype
- The second-highest score.
- Used for Pod composition balancing (a Builder-Operator can fill either role in a pinch).

### 4.3 Reassignment
- If Crucible behavior diverges significantly from pre-deployment Typing, the Archetype is reassigned.
- Reassignment happens in Week 7 (Reconstruction), after the Wall data is finalized.
- Pod composition may be adjusted (rare — only if the divergence makes the Pod non-functional).

### 4.4 No Self-Selection
- Xcitizens do not choose their Archetype. The data chooses it.
- An Xcitizen who believes they're a Pilot but whose data shows Builder is a Builder. Arguing with the data is arguing with your own behavior.
- Facilitators do not negotiate Archetype assignments. The data is the data.

## 5. ARCHETYPE DISTRIBUTION IN PODS

Every Pod must have all six Archetypes. Target composition for a 10-person Pod:

| Archetype | Count | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Pilot | 1 | Sets direction (one compass, not two) |
| Builder | 2–3 | Constructs (the majority — building is the primary activity) |
| Hustler | 1–2 | Closes deals (one primary, one backup) |
| Operator | 1–2 | Maintains (one primary, one backup) |
| Tracker | 1 | Surveils (one radar is enough; two compete) |
| Comms | 1 | Translates (one voice, one memory) |
| **Total** | **10** | |

The Pod Formation algorithm (see Document: Pod Formation Algorithm) composes Pods based on this target, with adjustments for ProtoCo-specific needs.

## 6. ARCHETYPE DEVELOPMENT DURING DEPLOYMENT

An Archetype is not a permanent label. It's a starting point. During the 24-month deployment, Xcitizens develop their secondary (and sometimes tertiary) Archetypes.

### 6.1 Primary Development
The primary Archetype deepens. The Builder becomes a better Builder. The Pilot becomes a better Pilot. This is the core of the Xcitizen's deployment contribution.

### 6.2 Secondary Development
The secondary Archetype strengthens. A Builder-Operator becomes more capable of maintaining systems, not just building them. This makes the Xcitizen more versatile and the Pod more resilient.

### 6.3 Cross-Archetype Exposure
During the deployment, Xcitizens are exposed to all six Archetypes (their Pod has all six). This builds appreciation and basic capability across all roles — even if the Xcitizen never becomes expert in them.

### 6.4 Post-Deployment Archetype Evolution
After the cliff (Month 24), Cycled Xcitizens carry their Archetype profile into their next venture. Many find that their secondary becomes their primary in a new context (a Builder-Operator who starts their own company may find they need to be a Pilot-Hustler). The Archetype is a starting point, not a ceiling.

## 7. ARCHETYPE-BASED KEEPER DETERMINATION

At the cliff (Month 24), the Keeper (top 3%) determination considers:
- Cumulative Gate performance
- Neural Link data
- Pod Leader assessment
- **Archetype contribution:** Did the Xcitizen fully embody their primary Archetype and contribute their secondary?

**No Archetype is favored for Keeper status.** A Pod needs all six. The Keeper determination is about excellence within the Archetype, not the Archetype itself. The best Builder, the best Pilot, the best Comms — all are equally eligible.

## 8. FACILITATOR CALIBRATION

### 8.1 Pre-Crucible Workshop
All facilitators attend a 2-day calibration workshop:
- Score the same recorded scenarios. Compare. Align on signals.
- Discuss edge cases. Agree on thresholds.

### 8.2 Continuous Calibration
- Daily during the Crucible: 15-minute co-facilitator sync. Compare notes. Flag discrepancies.
- Weekly: 60-minute calibration session. Review aggregate scores. Recalibrate if any facilitator is consistently higher/lower.

### 8.3 Bias Mitigation
- Each Xcitizen scored by 3+ facilitators across scenarios.
- Scores aggregated, not averaged by consensus (no groupthink).
- Facilitator names stripped from final scoring sheets.
- Quarterly audit: do certain facilitators consistently score higher/lower? Recalibrate.
