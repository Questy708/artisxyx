# 34. SLACK / DISCORD GUIDE

**xHansa Fellowship — Communication Platform Guide**

---

## 1. WHY SLACK (NOT DISCORD)

The Hansa League uses **Slack** as its primary communication platform. Slack is used for:
- Pod communication (private channels)
- Cohort-wide announcements
- Field-specific communities
- Direct messaging
- File sharing
- Integration with other League tools (Notion, venture tracker, Commons)

**Discord is NOT used** for official League communication. Some informal XCitizen communities may use Discord for side conversations (gaming, social), but no official business is conducted there. If you're invited to a Discord server, treat it as social — not operational.

---

## 2. SLACK STRUCTURE

### 2.1 Your Channel Map

When you log in to Slack on Day 1, you'll see these channels:

#### Pod Channels (Private — 10 members + Pod Leader)
- `#pod-[your-pod-id]` — Your Pod's private workspace. This is where 90% of your communication happens.

#### Cohort Channels
- `#cohort-[N]` — All 1,000 Xcitizens in your cohort. Announcements, social, cross-Pod questions.
- `#announcements` — Strategic Command announcements. **Read-only.** Read every message.
- `#commons-contributions` — Where Pods post weekly Commons contributions (Fridays by 18:00).

#### Field Channels
- `#field-water-systems` (or your assigned Field) — Cross-Pod discussion within your Civilizational Field. Share breakthroughs, ask for help, find collaborators.

#### Archetype Channels
- `#archetype-builders` (or your Archetype) — Connect with Xcitizens of the same Archetype across all Pods. Share Archetype-specific tips, tools, and patterns.

#### Support Channels
- `#support` — Tech support, logistics, general help. League staff monitor this.
- `#medical` — Confidential medical support. DM `@medical-team` for private matters.
- `#conduct` — Report Conduct violations to the Conduct Officer. Can DM `@conduct-officer` privately.

#### Social Channels (Optional)
- `#social` — Non-work chat. Memes, life, whatever.
- `#housing-[city]` — Housing-specific chat (logistics, roommate coordination).

### 2.2 Channels You Will NOT See
- Other Pods' private channels (by design — Pod privacy)
- Strategic Command internal channels
- Alumni-only channels (you gain access post-deployment)

## 3. SLACK CONFIGURATION

### 3.1 Profile Setup (Day 1)

- [ ] **Display name:** `[First Name] [Last Initial]` — `[Archetype]`
  - Example: `Amara O. — Builder`
- [ ] **Profile photo:** Clear headshot (not a logo, not an avatar)
- [ ] **Title:** `Xcitizen — Cohort [N] — [Field]`
- [ ] **Status:** Set to your current phase:
  - `🟠 Crucible — Week [X]` (during the Crucible)
  - `🔵 Q1 — Month [X]` (during Phase 1)
  - `🟢 Q2 — Month [X]` (during Phase 2)
  - `🟣 Q3 — Month [X]` (during Phase 3)
  - `🔴 Q4 — Month [X]` (during Phase 4)
- [ ] **Time zone:** Set to the XEmbassy's time zone (so timestamps are accurate)

### 3.2 Notification Settings

**Mobile push notifications:**
- **On** for: Your Pod channel, `#announcements`, `#support` (only when mentioned)
- **Off** for: All other channels (check manually)

**Do Not Disturb:**
- Set to **22:00–05:30** (your time zone)
- Exceptions: Your Pod Leader and Strategic Command can override DND

**Email digests:**
- Disable (you're on Slack enough that digests are noise)

### 3.3 Desktop Configuration
- [ ] Install Slack desktop app (not just browser)
- [ ] Set sidebar to "Unread + Mentions" (reduces noise)
- [ ] Enable "Mark as read" on scroll (keeps channels clean)
- [ ] Set theme to dark mode (easier on eyes during long sessions)

## 4. SLACK ETIQUETTE & PROTOCOLS

### 4.1 Response Times

| Channel Type | Expected Response Time |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Your Pod channel | Within 1 hour (during working hours) |
| Pod Leader DM | Within 2 hours |
| `#announcements` | Read within 4 hours |
| `#cohort-[N]` mentions | Within 4 hours |
| `#support` | Within 4 hours |
| Field / Archetype channels | Within 24 hours (not urgent) |
| Social channels | No expectation |

**Working hours:** 06:00–21:00 (XEmbassy time). After hours, respond by 08:00 the next day. **Exception:** The Crucible — respond immediately, always.

### 4.2 Communication Norms

**DO:**
- ✅ Use threads for discussions (keep the main channel clean)
- ✅ Be concise. One message, one point. If it's long, use a thread or a doc.
- ✅ Use `@here` for urgent Pod matters (everyone in the channel gets notified)
- ✅ Use `@channel` only for true emergencies (everyone, including DND, gets notified)
- ✅ Acknowledge receipt of important messages with ✅ or 👍
- ✅ Share wins and breakthroughs in the Commons-contributions channel
- ✅ Ask for help publicly (in your Pod or Field channel) — someone else may have the same question

**DON'T:**
- ❌ DM someone what should be in a channel (transparency is a League value)
- ❌ Tag `@channel` for non-emergencies (this is a fire alarm — don't pull it casually)
- ❌ Send a wall of text (use a thread, or a Notion doc, or a quick call)
- ❌ Screenshot and share other Pods' messages (Pod privacy)
- ❌ Discuss Gate results, Kill Switches, or Conduct matters in public channels (these go through Pod Leaders or the Conduct Officer)
- ❌ Use Slack for Confidential Information at the "Strategic Command" level (use Signal or in-person)

### 4.3 The Daily Standup (Async)

Every morning by 08:00, post your daily standup in your Pod channel:

```
**Standup — [Date]**
✅ Yesterday: [What you did]
→ Today: [What you're doing]
🚧 Blocked: [What's blocking you, or "Nothing"]
```

Your Pod Leader reads these. If you're blocked, they'll help. If you're not posting, they'll notice.

### 4.4 The Weekly Review (Sync)

Every week (day TBD by your Pod Leader), the Pod has a 60-minute sync review. This happens in person (at the XEmbassy) or on a Slack Huddle / video call. Agenda:
1. Progress vs. Gate trajectory (each member reports)
2. Blockers and risks
3. Commons contribution (what are we submitting this week?)
4. Next week's plan

Minutes are posted to the Pod channel in a thread.

## 5. SLACK FEATURES YOU'LL USE

### 5.1 Threads
- Start a thread by hovering over a message and clicking the reply icon.
- Keep discussions in threads. The main channel is for announcements and new topics.
- Threads can be named (click the thread, then "Edit name") — useful for tracking discussions.

### 5.2 Huddles
- Slack Huddles are quick voice calls within a channel.
- Start a huddle by clicking the headphones icon.
- Use for quick discussions that are faster to talk through than type.
- Your Pod may use huddles for daily check-ins during deployment.

### 5.3 File Sharing
- Drag and drop files into any channel or DM.
- Large files (>1GB) should go in Notion or the Commons, not Slack (Slack has storage limits).
- Shared files are accessible to everyone in the channel.

### 5.4 Integration with League Tools
Slack integrates with:
- **Notion:** Get notifications when docs are updated
- **Venture Tracker:** Gate submission notifications
- **League Commons:** Weekly contribution reminders
- **Google Calendar:** Pod meetings and Crucible schedule
- **TownSquare:** Cross-post important TownSquare threads to Slack

### 5.5 Saved Messages
- Star/bookmark important messages (announcements, Gate criteria, key decisions) for quick reference.
- Access saved items from the sidebar.

## 6. SECURITY ON SLACK

### 6.1 What You Can Share on Slack
- Pod operational discussions (your Pod channel is private)
- General cohort questions and social chat
- Commons contributions (the #commons-contributions channel is visible to the cohort)
- Non-sensitive Field discussion

### 6.2 What You CANNOT Share on Slack
- Strategic Command deliberations (use in-person or Signal)
- Other Xcitizens' biometric or medical data (never, on any channel)
- Gate criteria details (field-specific thresholds are Pod-private — discuss in person only)
- Anchor Partner contract terms (use email or in-person)
- Anything marked "Confidential — Strategic Command" (use Signal or in-person)

### 6.3 Slack Data Retention
- Pod channels: retained for the duration of the deployment + 12 months post-cliff
- Cohort channels: retained for the duration + 6 months post-cliff
- DMs: retained for 90 days
- The League may access Slack messages for Conduct investigations. Assume everything you write is readable by the Conduct Officer.

## 7. DISCORD (SOCIAL USE ONLY)

### 7.1 If You Use Discord
Some XCitizens maintain informal Discord servers for social purposes (gaming, music, off-topic chat). These are **not** League-operated and **not** monitored.

### 7.2 Rules for Discord Use
- No official League business on Discord. Ever.
- No Confidential Information on Discord (it's a third-party platform with unknown security).
- No discussion of ProtoCo details, Gate results, or other Xcitizens' information.
- If a Discord server becomes a channel for League business, Strategic Command will shut it down.

### 7.3 The League's Position
Discord is fine for social. It is not part of the Hansa communication infrastructure. If you need to communicate about deployment matters, use Slack, Signal, or in-person. Period.

---

## 8. TROUBLESHOOTING

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't access Slack | Contact IT via #support (or Hub Manager if Slack is down) |
| Notifications too loud | Adjust channel-specific settings (right-click channel → Change notifications) |
| Lost message history | Slack retains per Section 6.3. If you need older messages, contact IT. |
| Pod channel missing | Contact your Pod Leader — they can re-add you |
| Accidentally posted in wrong channel | Delete the message immediately (hover → delete). If it was Confidential Information, notify your Pod Leader. |
| Someone from outside the League messages you | Do not respond to League matters. Report to IT if it seems like social engineering. |

---

## 9. THE GOLDEN RULE

**Slack is a tool, not a substitute for operating.** If you're spending more time on Slack than building, you're doing it wrong. The best Xcitizens post their standup, respond to their Pod, contribute to the Commons weekly, and then go build. Slack is the nervous system, not the work.
