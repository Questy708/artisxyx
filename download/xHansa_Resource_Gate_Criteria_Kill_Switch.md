# xHANSA FELLOWSHIP — GATE CRITERIA & KILL SWITCH PROTOCOL

**INTERNAL — STRATEGIC COMMAND + POD LEADERSHIP USE**

---

## 1. THE GATE SYSTEM — OVERVIEW

The Gate System is the operational backbone of the xHansa deployment. There are 5 Gates (Gate 0 at the end of the Crucible, then Gates 1–4 at the end of each quarterly phase). Each Gate is binary: pass or fail. There is no subjective evaluation, no committee vote, no appeals process.

| Gate | Timing | Phase | Pass Criterion | Fail Consequence |
|------|--------|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| Gate 0 | End of Crucible (Week 8) | Onboarding | Crucible certification | Xcitizen does not deploy |
| Gate 1 | Month 6 | Q1: 0-to-1 Pilot | Minimal Version + Anchor LOI | Kill Switch |
| Gate 2 | Month 12 | Q2: First Revenue | Revenue + unit economics | Kill Switch |
| Gate 3 | Month 18 | Q3: Expansion | Replication + Playbook | Kill Switch |
| Gate 4 | Month 24 | Q4: Institutionalization | 80%+ operational efficiency | The Cliff (spin-out or dissolution) |

---

## 2. GATE 0 — CRUCIBLE CERTIFICATION

**Timing:** End of Week 8 (the final week of the Crucible)
**Assessed by:** Crucible instructors + Neural Link Protocol data
**Type:** Individual (not Pod-level)

### Pass Criteria
All four must be met:
1. **Attendance:** Present for ≥95% of Crucible sessions (no more than 2 absences)
2. **Behavioral certification:** Observed execution-first behavior under sustained cognitive exhaustion (The Wall). Assessed via:
   - Did the candidate continue executing when spent?
   - Did the candidate default to direction (Pilot), construction (Builder), closing (Hustler), maintenance (Operator), surveillance (Tracker), or translation (Comms)?
   - Did the candidate exhibit compliance conditioning (defer, wait, ask permission) or execution capability (decide, act, report)?
3. **Archetype stability:** The Archetype assigned in pre-deployment Typing is confirmed by Crucible behavior. If behavior diverges significantly, the Archetype is reassigned (and Pod composition may be adjusted).
4. **Physical/cognitive recovery:** Neural Link biometric data shows the candidate can recover from cognitive exhaustion (heart rate variability, sleep patterns return to baseline within 48 hours of The Wall).

### Fail Consequence
The Xcitizen does not deploy. Options:
- **Recycle:** Defer to the next cohort's Crucible (rare, only if the failure is physical recovery, not behavioral)
- **Release:** Severed with no equity (the Crucible is Gate 0 — no equity has been earned yet)

**There is no appeals process.** The decision of the Crucible instructors is final.

## 3. GATE 1 — 0-TO-1 PILOT (MONTH 6)

**Timing:** End of Month 6
**Assessed by:** Strategic Command + Anchor Partner verification
**Type:** Pod-level (the entire Pod passes or fails together)

### Pass Criteria (ALL must be met)

#### 3.1 Minimal Version Deployed
The Pod must have built and deployed a **functioning asset** in the field. "Functioning" means:
- It works (not a prototype, not a mockup — a deployed, operating thing)
- It is in the hands of real users (not internal testers)
- It has run for ≥30 days in the field without critical failure

**Evidence required:**
- Photos/video of the deployed asset in operation
- Logs/usage data from the 30-day field period
- User testimony (≥5 real users interviewed)

#### 3.2 Anchor Partner LOI Signed
The Pod must have secured a signed Letter of Intent from an institutional partner (a government agency, NGO, corporation, or distributor) committing to pilot or adopt the ProtoCo.

**LOI requirements:**
- On institutional letterhead
- Signed by an authorized signatory (not a "partnership manager" — someone with budget authority)
- Specifies the pilot scope, geography, and timeline
- Non-binding but specific (not a "we're interested in exploring" letter)

#### 3.3 Operational Baseline
The Pod must demonstrate:
- A functioning build/deploy workflow (using XEmbassy infrastructure)
- A working relationship with at least 1 supplier or vendor
- A documented operating procedure for the Minimal Version

### Fail Consequence: The Kill Switch
See Section 7.

## 4. GATE 2 — FIRST REVENUE (MONTH 12)

**Timing:** End of Month 12
**Assessed by:** Strategic Command + financial audit
**Type:** Pod-level

### Pass Criteria (ALL must be met)

#### 4.1 Revenue
The Pod must have generated **actual revenue** (not projections, not LOIs, not pilot grants). Revenue thresholds are field-specific and set at commissioning:

| Field | Minimum Revenue (Month 12) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Water Systems | $2,000/month MRR |
| Energy Systems | $3,000/month MRR |
| Food & Agriculture | $2,500/month MRR |
| Health & Mobility | $3,000/month MRR |
| Built Environment | $5,000/month MRR (project-based) |
| Data & Intelligence | $4,000/month MRR |
| Logistics & Trade | $5,000/month MRR (transaction volume) |
| Governance & Identity | $3,000/month MRR |
| Education & Human Capital | $2,000/month MRR |

*These are minimums. Pods are encouraged to exceed them significantly.*

#### 4.2 Unit Economics
All four metrics must hit threshold:

| Metric | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) | < LTV / 3 |
| LTV (Lifetime Value) | > 3 × CAC |
| Churn | < 10% monthly (for subscription) or < 20% (for transactional) |
| Throughput | Field-specific (set at commissioning) |

**If any single metric fails, the Gate fails.** There is no "close enough."

#### 4.3 Operational Stability
- No critical operational failure in the last 60 days
- Supplier/vendor relationships stable (no single point of failure)
- Team health: Neural Link data shows Pod is not in burnout cascade

### Fail Consequence: The Kill Switch

## 5. GATE 3 — EXPANSION (MONTH 18)

**Timing:** End of Month 18
**Assessed by:** Strategic Command + geographic audit
**Type:** Pod-level

### Pass Criteria (ALL must be met)

#### 5.1 Replication
The ProtoCo must be successfully deployed in **≥3 new geographies** beyond the pilot site. "Successfully deployed" means:
- The asset is operating in each geography
- Each geography has ≥1 local operator (can be a partner, not yet a permanent hire)
- Each geography has ≥5 real users

**Geographic diversity requirement:** The 3+ geographies must have meaningfully different constraints (regulatory, supply chain, customer behavior). Deploying to 3 similar neighborhoods in the same city does not count.

#### 5.2 The Playbook
The Pod must author and deliver the **permanent Playbook** — a codified, step-by-step operational manual that any future operator can execute without the original team.

**Playbook requirements:**
- Covers: setup, operations, maintenance, troubleshooting, scaling, local adaptation
- ≥50 pages
- Includes: process diagrams, decision trees, vendor lists, regulatory checklists
- Tested: a non-team member (an XCitizen from another Pod) must be able to follow it and operate the ProtoCo for 1 day without assistance

#### 5.3 Replication Economics
Unit economics in the new geographies must match or exceed the pilot site (within 20%). If the model doesn't transfer economically, it doesn't transfer.

### Fail Consequence: The Kill Switch

## 6. GATE 4 — INSTITUTIONALIZATION & THE CLIFF (MONTH 24)

**Timing:** End of Month 24
**Assessed by:** Strategic Command + operational audit
**Type:** Pod-level + Individual

### Pass Criteria (Pod-level)

#### 6.1 Local Operator Transition
- Local permanent operators recruited and trained in each geography
- Local operators have run the ProtoCo independently for ≥30 days
- Original Pod members are in advisory/transition roles, not operational roles

#### 6.2 Operational Efficiency
All KPIs must exceed **80%** under local operator control:
- Uptime/reliability > 80%
- Revenue retention > 80% (vs. Pod-operated period)
- Customer satisfaction > 80%
- Cost efficiency > 80% (cost per unit vs. Pod-operated period)

### The Cliff (Individual-level Outcome)

At Month 24, regardless of Gate 4 outcome, the deployment ends for all Xcitizens. The stipend stops instantly. No grace period.

| Outcome | Who | What happens |
|---------|-----|--------------|
| **Keepers** (top 3%) | Highest-performing Xcitizens across the cohort | Transition to permanent payroll within the spun-out entity. Salary, benefits, equity acceleration. |
| **The Cycled** (remaining 97%) | All other Xcitizens | Severed with performance equity grants (36-month vesting). XCitizen status retained. Network access retained. No salary. |

**Gate 4 failure (Pod-level):** If the Pod fails Gate 4, the venture does not spin out. Assets are absorbed into the League Commons. All Xcitizens in the Pod are cycled out (the 3% Keeper designation does not apply to failed-Pod members). This is rare — Pods that reach Gate 4 have usually passed Gates 1–3 and are operationally sound.

## 7. THE KILL SWITCH — PROTOCOL

### Trigger
A Pod fails Gate 1, 2, or 3.

### Timeline
| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| T+0 hr | Gate assessment complete. Fail determination logged. |
| T+1 hr | Pod Leader notified. Strategic Command convenes. |
| T+4 hr | Pod members individually notified. No group meeting (prevents collusion/negotiation). |
| T+24 hr | Assets (IP, code, hardware, vendor relationships) inventoried and transferred to League Commons. |
| T+48 hr | Pod formally dissolved. Xcitizens reassigned. |

### Xcitizen Reassignment Options
1. **Reassign to another Pod** (if a Pod has an open slot due to attrition and the Xcitizen's Archetype fits) — most common for Gate 1 failures
2. **Reassign to support role** (XEmbassy operations, League Commons, Strategic Command support) — common for Gate 2 failures
3. **Cycle out** (severed with pro-rated performance equity, 36-month vesting) — common for Gate 3 failures, or if the Xcitizen's performance contributed to the failure

**Reassignment is not a punishment.** It acknowledges that a Pod failed, not necessarily the individuals in it. However, Xcitizens who were the primary cause of failure (determined by Neural Link data + Pod Leader assessment) are more likely to be cycled out.

### No Appeals
There is no appeals process. The Gate is binary. The data is the data. This is non-negotiable — the clarity of the Gate system is what makes it work. Introduce appeals, and you introduce politics.

---

## 8. REPORTING & TRANSPARENCY

- Gate results are published to the entire cohort within 24 hours of determination
- Kill Switch events are announced (Pod ID, Gate failed, date) — transparency is a feature, not a bug
- Individual Xcitizen reassignment outcomes are private (between the Xcitizen and Strategic Command)

---

## 9. ESCALATION

The only escalation path is for **process errors** (e.g., the Gate was assessed incorrectly, data was wrong), not for **outcome disputes** (e.g., "we think we should have passed"). Process error claims are reviewed by Strategic Command within 7 days and, if validated, result in a re-assessment — not an automatic pass.
