# xHANSA FELLOWSHIP — COHORT OPERATIONS RUNBOOK

**The week-by-week playbook for running a cohort. Strategic Command + Pod Leadership use.**

---

## PHASE 0: PRE-DEPLOYMENT (WEEKS -4 TO 0)

### Week -4: Final Prep
- [ ] Pod formation algorithm run; 100 Pods composed
- [ ] Each Xcitizen receives: Pod assignment, ProtoCo commission, XEmbassy location, deployment start date
- [ ] XEmbassy logistics confirmed (housing, workspace, equipment)
- [ ] Crucible instructors finalized and briefed
- [ ] Neural Link devices provisioned (1,000+ units)
- [ ] Medical support team on standby for the Crucible

### Week -3: Xcitizen Arrival
- [ ] Xcitizens travel to assigned XEmbassies
- [ ] Housing check-in
- [ ] XEmbassy access credentials issued
- [ ] Pre-deployment medical screening (baseline biometrics for Neural Link)
- [ ] Deployment Agreements signed (see Onboarding resource)

### Week -2: Settle + Brief
- [ ] XEmbassy orientation (facilities, rules, emergency protocols)
- [ ] League Commons briefing (IP, how to contribute, how to access)
- [ ] Gate System briefing (Gate 0–4 criteria, Kill Switch)
- [ ] Pod rosters distributed (Xcitizens learn who their Pod is — but Pods do NOT meet yet)
- [ ] Stipend payments confirmed (first payment on Day 1 of Crucible)

### Week -1: Final Briefing
- [ ] ProtoCo commission briefings (each Pod receives its commission document)
- [ ] Field orientation (domain primer for each Pod's assigned field)
- [ ] Crucible logistics briefing (schedule, what to expect, what to bring)
- [ ] **Rest.** The Crucible begins in 1 week. Xcitizens are told to sleep, eat, and prepare.

## PHASE 0: THE CRUCIBLE (WEEKS 1–8)

### Week 1: The Shock Begins
**Theme:** Systematic destabilization of habitual thought patterns.

| Day | Activity | Purpose |
|-----|----------|---------|
| 1 | Pods meet for the first time. Pod Leaders introduced. | First-impression dynamics observed |
| 2 | "The Shock" begins. Xcitizens are given ambiguous, high-pressure tasks with no clear instructions and shifting constraints. | Strip away compliance conditioning (the habit of waiting for permission) |
| 3 | Tasks escalate. Information is deliberately incomplete. Deadlines are impossibly tight. | Force execution-first decision-making |
| 4 | Pods are restructured mid-task (members swapped temporarily). | Test adaptability; prevent premature role-locking |
| 5 | Individual debriefs. "What did you do? What should you have done?" | Self-awareness assessment |
| 6 | Rest half-day. Then a longer, more complex scenario. | Observe behavior under fatigue onset |
| 7 | Reflection day. Journaling, 1-on-1s with Crucible instructors. | Integration |

**Neural Link monitoring:** Baseline established. Stress indicators tracked. Any Xcitizen showing dangerous physiological response is pulled aside by the medical team.

**Pod Leader role:** Observe, do not lead. Pod Leaders intervene only for safety.

### Week 2–3: The Shock Deepens
- Scenarios increase in complexity and ambiguity
- Pods are given ProtoCo-adjacent problems (not their actual ProtoCo — that comes later)
- Cross-Pod collaboration is encouraged and observed
- Archetype behaviors begin to stabilize (who leads, who builds, who closes, who tracks)
- **Mid-Crucible assessment (end of Week 4):** Interim Archetype scoring. Adjustments to Pod composition if behavior diverges significantly from pre-deployment Typing.

### Week 4–5: The Wall
**Theme:** Sustained cognitive exhaustion.

- Sleep is restricted (not eliminated — restricted to 5–6 hours)
- Tasks are relentless — no breaks between scenarios
- The pace is sustained for 10–14 days
- This is the phase that separates those who can operate when spent from those who cannot

**Neural Link monitoring:** Intensive. Heart rate variability, sleep patterns, cognitive load tracked continuously. Medical team on 24/7 standby. **Any Xcitizen showing dangerous fatigue is pulled for mandatory rest** — the Wall is intense, not unsafe.

**Behavioral ground-truth emerges:** By the end of The Wall, the Archetype assignments are confirmed. Who leads when the plan fails, who stabilizes the group, who identifies the exit — these are now clear.

### Week 6–7: Reconstruction
**Theme:** Rebuild with execution-first cognitive architecture.

- The pace eases (sleep restored to 7–8 hours)
- Xcitizens are taught the xCelero operating model: how Pods work, how the League Commons works, how the Gate System works
- Pods begin working on their actual ProtoCo commissions (scoping, not building yet)
- The shift from "survive the Crucible" to "prepare to deploy"
- Archetype assignments finalized. Pod composition frozen.

### Week 8: Gate 0 — Certification
- **Days 1–4:** Final scenarios + debriefs
- **Day 5:** Gate 0 assessment. Each Xcitizen evaluated individually:
  - Attendance (≥95%)
  - Behavioral certification (execution-first under exhaustion)
  - Archetype stability
  - Physical/cognitive recovery (Neural Link data)
- **Day 6:** Gate 0 results announced. Xcitizens who pass are certified for deployment. Xcitizens who fail are recycled (deferred to next cohort) or released.
- **Day 7:** Rest. Deployment begins the next day.

**Crucible attrition rate (historical):** ~10–15% do not pass Gate 0. This is expected. The Crucible is designed to filter.

## PHASE 1: Q1 — 0-TO-1 PILOT (MONTHS 1–6)

### Month 1: Scope + Mobilize
- [ ] Pod receives full ProtoCo brief (problem, target geography, Anchor Partner target, Gate 1 requirements)
- [ ] Pod defines the Minimal Version (smallest deployable thing)
- [ ] Build plan created (milestones, materials, vendor list)
- [ ] XEmbassy resources allocated (lab/foundry/studio time, equipment)
- [ ] Anchor Partner outreach begins (if not pre-assigned)
- [ ] Weekly Pod Leader check-ins begin
- [ ] **Neural Link:** Baseline for deployment phase established

### Month 2–3: Build Sprint
- [ ] Minimal Version construction begins
- [ ] Vendor relationships established (materials, components, services)
- [ ] Weekly build reviews (Pod Leader + Strategic Command rep)
- [ ] Anchor Partner negotiation intensifies (LOI target: end of Month 4)
- [ ] First internal demo (Month 3) — Pod shows the work-in-progress to Strategic Command
- [ ] **Kill Switch watch:** If the Pod is stalled (no build progress for 2 weeks), Strategic Command intervenes with a "reset or dissolve" warning

### Month 4–5: Field Prep + LOI
- [ ] Minimal Version reaches "deployable" state
- [ ] Anchor Partner LOI signed (Month 4 target; Month 5 latest)
- [ ] Field deployment site prepared (user recruitment, logistics)
- [ ] Pre-deployment testing (internal — does it work before real users touch it?)
- [ ] **Month 5 check-in:** Strategic Command reviews Gate 1 trajectory. If LOI is not secured by end of Month 5, the Pod is warned: "Gate 1 in 30 days. LOI required."

### Month 6: Deploy + Gate 1
- [ ] **Week 1:** Minimal Version deployed to field (real users)
- [ ] **Weeks 2–4:** 30-day field operation period (logs, usage data, user feedback)
- [ ] **Week 4:** Gate 1 assessment:
  - Minimal Version deployed and operating for ≥30 days? ✓/✗
  - Anchor Partner LOI signed? ✓/✗
  - Operational baseline established? ✓/✗
- [ ] **Gate 1 result:** Pass → advance to Q2. Fail → Kill Switch (see Gate Criteria resource).

---

## PHASE 2: Q2 — FIRST REVENUE (MONTHS 7–12)

### Month 7–8: Revenue Activation
- [ ] Monetization model finalized (pricing, payment mechanism)
- [ ] First paying customers acquired (target: 10+ in Month 7, 25+ by Month 8)
- [ ] Revenue tracking system live (MRR, CAC, LTV, churn)
- [ ] Weekly revenue reviews begin

### Month 9–10: Metrics Debugging
- [ ] Unit economics analyzed (are CAC < LTV? is churn within threshold?)
- [ ] Technical failures debugged in real-time
- [ ] Operational gaps patched (supply chain, customer support, logistics)
- [ ] If metrics are below Gate 2 threshold, the Pod pivots or doubles down (Strategic Command advisory)

### Month 11–12: Stabilize + Gate 2
- [ ] Revenue stabilized at or above Gate 2 threshold (field-specific; see Gate Criteria resource)
- [ ] Unit economics sustainable (CAC < LTV/3, LTV > 3×CAC, churn < 10%)
- [ ] **Month 12, Week 4:** Gate 2 assessment:
  - Revenue at/above threshold? ✓/✗
  - Unit economics met? ✓/✗
  - Operational stability (no critical failure in 60 days)? ✓/✗
- [ ] **Gate 2 result:** Pass → advance to Q3. Fail → Kill Switch.

---

## PHASE 3: Q3 — EXPANSION (MONTHS 13–18)

### Month 13–14: Site Selection
- [ ] 3–5 new geographies identified for replication (must have meaningfully different constraints)
- [ ] Local partners/operators identified in each geography
- [ ] Replication plan authored (what transfers, what needs local adaptation)
- [ ] Logistics for multi-site deployment arranged

### Month 15–16: Parallel Deployment
- [ ] Minimal Version deployed in 3+ new geographies simultaneously
- [ ] Local operators trained (using a draft of the Playbook)
- [ ] Cross-geography data collection begins (does the model transfer?)

### Month 17–18: Playbook + Gate 3
- [ ] **Playbook authored** (≥50 pages: setup, operations, maintenance, troubleshooting, scaling, local adaptation)
- [ ] Playbook tested (an XCitizen from another Pod follows it for 1 day without assistance)
- [ ] Replication economics confirmed (unit economics in new geographies within 20% of pilot)
- [ ] **Month 18, Week 4:** Gate 3 assessment:
  - 3+ geographies deployed and operating? ✓/✗
  - Playbook delivered and tested? ✓/✗
  - Replication economics met? ✓/✗
- [ ] **Gate 3 result:** Pass → advance to Q4. Fail → Kill Switch.

---

## PHASE 4: Q4 — INSTITUTIONALIZATION (MONTHS 19–24)

### Month 19–20: Local Operator Recruitment
- [ ] Permanent local operators recruited in each geography (replacing temporary partners)
- [ ] Operators trained on the Playbook
- [ ] Pod members transition to advisory/mentorship roles (not operational)

### Month 21–22: Handover
- [ ] Local operators run the ProtoCo independently for ≥30 days
- [ ] Pod members available for consultation but not intervention
- [ ] Operational efficiency measured (is it >80% under local control?)

### Month 23: Efficiency Audit
- [ ] All KPIs audited: uptime >80%, revenue retention >80%, satisfaction >80%, cost efficiency >80%
- [ ] If any KPI is below 80%, the Pod has 30 days to fix it (with local operator involvement)
- [ ] **Keeper/Cycled preliminary assessment** begins (Strategic Command reviews all Xcitizens)

### Month 24: The Cliff
- [ ] **Week 1–2:** Final Gate 4 assessment
- [ ] **Week 3:** Keeper/Cycled determinations finalized. Individual meetings with each Xcitizen.
- [ ] **Week 4 (final day of Month 24):** The Cliff.
  - Stipend stops.
  - Keepers (3%) transition to permanent payroll in the spun-out entity.
  - The Cycled (97%) are severed with performance equity grants (36-month vesting).
  - Venture officially spins out as an independent entity (or, if Gate 4 failed, assets absorbed into League Commons).
- [ ] **Cohort closing ceremony** (optional — not all Xcitizens will want to attend; respect that).

---

## POST-CLIFF (MONTH 25+)

- [ ] Spun-out ventures onboarded to the xCelero venture portfolio (ongoing support)
- [ ] Cycled Xcitizens: performance equity grants documented; 36-month vesting schedules activated
- [ ] All Xcitizens: XCitizen status retained; TownSquare access retained
- [ ] Debrief interviews (anonymous, for algorithm + program improvement)
- [ ] Cohort retrospective: what worked, what failed, what to change for next cohort
- [ ] **Cohort N+1 applications open** (if not already)

## DAILY / WEEKLY RHYTHMS (THROUGHOUT DEPLOYMENT)

### Daily
- **Pod standup** (15 min, async on Slack — what did you do, what are you doing, what's blocked)
- **Neural Link data** synced (automatic)
- **Pod Leader available** for ad-hoc consultation

### Weekly
- **Pod review** (60 min with Pod Leader): progress vs. Gate trajectory, blockers, risks
- **Strategic Command check-in** (Pod Leader attends, reports on all Pods in their hub): any Pods off-track?
- **Friday cohort update** (all Pods): shared wins, shared failures, League Commons contributions
- **Kill Switch watch** (Strategic Command): any Pods trending toward Gate failure? Early intervention.

### Monthly
- **Gate trajectory review** (all Pods): are they on track for the next Gate?
- **Neural Link health review** (medical team): any burnout cascades? Any Xcitizens needing support?
- **Financial audit** (Strategic Command): stipend payments, venture revenue, budget burn

### Quarterly
- **Gate assessment** (all Pods): the binary pass/fail
- **Cohort-wide retrospective** (post-Gate): what did we learn this quarter?
- **Algorithm feedback** (Strategic Command): any Pod-formation or Gate-criteria adjustments needed?
