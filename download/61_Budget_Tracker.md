## PURPOSE

The Budget Tracker monitors the Pod's financial performance against its allocated budget. It is maintained by the Operator, reviewed weekly, and submitted monthly to Strategic Command. Budget overruns are a risk to Gate passage — the Tracker must be accurate and current.

---

## BUDGET RULES

1. **The Operator owns it.** They log expenses within 7 days, update the summary weekly, and present in the Monthly Review.
2. **Pilot approval for > $50.** Pod Leader approval for > $2,000. Strategic Command for > $5,000. No exceptions — unauthorized expenses are not reimbursed.
3. **Receipts for > $20.** Submit via the venture tracker. Lost receipts: up to $50 with signed declaration; over $50 not reimbursable.
4. **Per diem: $30/day** for travel meals/incidentals. No receipt needed. Paid on next stipend.
5. **Budget overruns are a risk.** If the Pod is trending over budget, the Operator flags it in the Weekly Review. The Pilot adjusts. If the adjustment isn't enough, the Pod Leader intervenes.
6. **Budget is not a suggestion.** The Studio Fund allocates a fixed amount per Pod. Overruns may mean the Pod cannot complete the phase — which means a Gate failure. Manage it.

---

## BUDGET OVERFLOW PROTOCOL

If the Pod is projected to exceed its phase budget:

1. **Week 1 of trend:** The Operator flags it in the Weekly Review. The Pilot reviews expenses and identifies cuts.
2. **Week 2 of trend:** If cuts aren't enough, the Pod Leader is notified. A revised budget is drafted.
3. **Week 3 of trend:** If the revised budget still exceeds allocation, the Pod Leader escalates to Strategic Command. Options:
   - **Supplemental allocation** (rare — requires strong justification)
   - **Scope reduction** (do less with the same budget — the Pilot cuts scope)
   - **Gate risk** (if the budget gap can't be closed, the Gate is at risk — this is a Kill Switch risk)

**Better to flag early and adjust than to hide it and fail the Gate.** Radical honesty applies to finances too.
