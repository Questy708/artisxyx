# xHANSA FELLOWSHIP — APPLICANT FAQ

**The honest answers. Read this before you apply.**

---

## THE BASICS

### What is the xHansa Fellowship?
A 24-month human capital deployment engine. We intake 1,000 Xcitizens per cohort, deploy them as 100 ten-person Pods, and each Pod builds one commissioned ProtoCo across one of Nine Civilizational Fields. It is not an accelerator, a course, a bootcamp, or a job. It is a military-grade knowledge-deployment pipeline.

### Is this an accelerator?
No. Accelerators take existing companies and help them grow. xHansa takes operators and deploys them to build companies from scratch — inside xCelero's infrastructure, with xCelero's capital, against xCelero-commissioned ProtoCos. You don't apply with a company. You apply as an operator.

### Is this a job?
No. Xcitizens are not employees. You receive a needs-based stipend ($500–$1,200/month), not a salary. No benefits. No guaranteed employment after 24 months. You are mission-deployed personnel whose compensation is tied to operational outcomes, not time served.

### Is this an educational program?
No. You will learn, but learning is not the purpose. The purpose is to build operational ventures. If you want a course, this is not it. If you want to build, it is.

---

## THE COMMITMENT

### How long is it?
24 months. No exit option before the cliff at Month 24.

### What happens if I leave early?
Total forfeiture of all equity and performance grants. The 24-month cliff exists precisely because infrastructure cannot be built by people who have an easy exit. Early departures are rare and economically punitive.

### Can I have another job or commitment during the fellowship?
No. The fellowship is full-time. The Crucible alone will consume every waking hour for 8 weeks. After that, the deployment is relentless. If you cannot commit 100%, do not apply.

### Can I apply if I have a family or dependents?
Yes, but be honest with yourself about the demands. The stipend is needs-based (up to $1,200/month for those with dependents). The 24-month commitment and the intensity of the Crucible are non-negotiable. We do not make accommodations that compromise the deployment.

## THE COMPENSATION

### How much do I get paid?
A needs-based stipend of $500–$1,200/month. The exact amount is determined by your circumstances (location, dependents, cost of living). It is tied to operational outcomes, not time served.

### Is there equity?
Yes — performance equity grants with 36-month vesting. But: individual IP is subordinate to the League Commons. You don't own what you build; the League owns it, and you share in the upside through equity.

### What happens at Month 24?
The cliff. The top 3% of Xcitizens — the "Keepers" — transition to permanent payroll within the spun-out entity. The remaining 97% — "The Cycled" — are severed with performance equity grants (36-month vesting). This is not a failure. It is the design. The League builds ventures, not careers.

### Is the 97% who are cycled out "failing"?
No. The venture they built survives. They carry equity, network, operational experience, and the XCitizen status into whatever they build next. The model is designed so that the venture outlives the Pod that built it. If you want lifetime employment, this is the wrong program.

## THE CRUCIBLE

### What is the Crucible?
An 8-week psychological demolition and reconstruction process. It overwrites compliance conditioning with execution capability. It consists of "The Shock" (systematic destabilization of habitual thought patterns) and "The Wall" (sustained cognitive exhaustion that strips away social performance).

### Is it safe?
It is intense by design. It is not hazing. It is a controlled environment run by experienced operators. We monitor biometric and cognitive load throughout (the Neural Link Protocol). But it will be the hardest thing you have ever done. If that prospect excites you, apply. If it terrifies you, also apply — but know what you're signing up for.

### What happens if I don't complete the Crucible?
You do not deploy. There is no appeals process. The Crucible is Gate 0.

### Can I prepare for it?
Not really. You can be physically fit, well-rested, and mentally committed. Beyond that, the Crucible is designed to be un-preparable-for. It tests who you are when the plan fails, not what you've memorized.

## THE PODS

### How are Pods formed?
A deterministic algorithm weighs your Deployment Classification (field) and Archetype compatibility. The Civilizational Field Typing Assessment observes your behavior under stress — not your stated preference — to assign you one of six Archetypes: Pilot, Builder, Hustler, Operator, Tracker, or Comms. The algorithm then composes 10-person Pods to maximize complementary strengths.

### Can I choose my Pod?
No. Pod formation is algorithmic. You can note preferences, but the algorithm decides. This is non-negotiable — the composition of Pods is too important to leave to social preference.

### Can I switch Pods?
Only via the Kill Switch (if your Pod is dissolved) or reassignment by Strategic Command. You cannot request a switch because you don't get along with your Pod. You will learn to operate with anyone. That is the point.

### What are the Six Archetypes?
- **Pilot** — sets direction; sees the path before others
- **Builder** — constructs; turns ideas into working things
- **Hustler** — closes; gets the deal, the partner, the customer
- **Operator** — maintains; keeps the system running when others are spent
- **Tracker** — surveils; finds the data, the signal, the risk before it hits
- **Comms** — translates; makes the complex legible

Every Pod has all six. No Pod is complete without them.

## THE GATES

### What are the Gates?
Quarterly performance gates with binary pass/fail outcomes. Each Gate has precise mathematical thresholds. No subjective evaluation. No committee votes. Either your Pod hits the numbers or it doesn't.

### What are the Gate thresholds?
They are field-specific and disclosed to each Pod at deployment. They cover: Minimal Version deployed (Gate 1), revenue and unit economics (Gate 2), replication and Playbook (Gate 3), operational efficiency >80% (Gate 4). The exact numbers are not public — they are commissioned per ProtoCo.

### What happens if my Pod fails a Gate?
The Kill Switch. The Pod is dissolved within 48 hours. Assets are redistributed. Xcitizens are reassigned (to other Pods, to support roles, or — in some cases — cycled out). There is no appeals process and no extension.

### Is the Kill Switch really 48 hours?
Yes. The speed is intentional. Lingering failure corrodes morale and wastes capital. A clean cut lets everyone move forward.

## THE FIELDS

### What are the Nine Civilizational Fields?
1. Water Systems
2. Energy Systems
3. Food & Agriculture
4. Health & Mobility
5. Built Environment
6. Data & Intelligence
7. Logistics & Trade
8. Governance & Identity
9. Education & Human Capital

Every ProtoCo is commissioned against one of these fields.

### Can I choose my field?
You express preferences in your application. The Typing Assessment and the commissioning process determine your assignment. We weigh your preference, but the needs of the commission come first.

### What is a ProtoCo?
A commissioned venture concept. Each Pod receives a ProtoCo — a specific venture to build within a specific field. You don't bring your own idea; you execute a commission. (If you have your own idea, apply to Inception Studios instead.)

## THE IP

### Who owns what I build?
The League. Individual IP is subordinate to the League Commons (the Supremacy Clause). Breakthroughs are shared across the network. When one Pod cracks a supply chain problem in Lagos, every Pod in Accra, Nairobi, and Cairo benefits immediately.

### Why would I give up my IP?
Because you get something better: the entire Hansa network's IP, infrastructure, and intelligence. The Commons accelerates every venture by eliminating redundant problem-solving. You give up one idea; you gain a thousand.

### Can I start my own company after the fellowship?
Yes — but not with the IP you built at xHansa. That belongs to the League. You can, however, spin out as a Keeper (top 3%) and join the permanent payroll of the venture you built. Or you can build something new, with the network and experience you gained.

## THE APPLICATION

### Who should apply?
- Informal-economy operators and grassroots workers
- Self-taught engineers who thrive under resource constraint
- Builders willing to surrender individual IP for the League Commons
- People who can commit to 24 months with no exit before the cliff
- Those who believe in collective intelligence over individual heroism

### Who should NOT apply?
- Anyone who needs a salary, benefits, or guaranteed employment
- Anyone who cannot relocate to an XEmbassy for 24 months
- Anyone who prioritizes individual IP over collective infrastructure
- Anyone looking for a "program" to pad their résumé

### Do I need a degree?
No. We do not select on credentials, university, or work history. We select on the evidence that you operate — and that you operate for something larger than yourself.

### Do I need a company or an idea?
No. You apply as an operator. The ProtoCo is commissioned to you.

### What's the selection process?
1. Application (this form)
2. Civilizational Field Typing Assessment (behavioral, under stress — not an interview)
3. Deployment Classification (field + Archetype assignment)
4. Pod Formation (algorithmic)
5. The Crucible (8 weeks)
6. Deployment (24 months)

### When do applications open?
Cohort 3, 2025: March 1 – May 15, 2025. Deployment begins July 2025.
Cohort 4, 2026: March 1 – May 15, 2026.

### Is there an application fee?
No. The fellowship is fully funded. You pay nothing to apply or participate.

## THE HONEST PART

### Is this program right for me?
If you read this entire FAQ and felt a mix of excitement and dread — yes.
If you skimmed it looking for the catch — probably not.
If you're looking for the catch so you can decide whether to accept it — yes, absolutely.

### What's the catch?
There isn't one. The terms are on the surface: 24 months, $500–$1,200/month, no exit, IP to the Commons, binary Gates, a 3%/97% cliff. The question is not whether the deal is fair — it is whether you are the kind of operator who takes it.
