## PURPOSE

The Performance Tracker is your personal dashboard during the Crucible. It helps you track your own development across the six Archetype dimensions, monitor your health metrics, and prepare for Gate 0. It is NOT the official assessment (the facilitators maintain that) — it is your self-tracking tool for self-awareness.

**Update it daily** during the evening reflection period (20:00–21:30).

|------|------------|--------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------|-----------|-------|

### Scale Meanings
- **Sleep Quality:** 1 = terrible, 5 = excellent
- **Energy:** 1 = exhausted, 5 = fully charged
- **Stress:** 1 = calm, 5 = overwhelmed
- **Mood:** 1 = low, 5 = high

### Red Flags to Watch For
- **Sleep < 5 hours for 3+ consecutive nights** → You're in the Wall. Push through, but tell the medical team if you feel unsafe.
- **Energy AM = 1 for 3+ consecutive days** → You may be burning out. Self-refer to the medical team.
- **Stress = 5 for 3+ consecutive days** → You're in the red zone. Talk to the medical team or your Pod Leader.
- **Mood = 1 for 3+ consecutive days** → This is not weakness. This is data. Talk to someone.

### The Medical Team Can See Your Neural Link Data
They know your HRV, your sleep, your stress indicators. If they pull you for rest, it's because the data says you need it. Don't argue. Rest. Return.

## SECTION 6: THE KEEPER QUESTION

From Week 6 onward, reflect on this question weekly:

> "If I were assessing myself for Keeper (top 3%) vs. Cycled (97%), based on my Crucible performance, what would I determine? And is that determination based on evidence — or on ego?"

This is not about ambition. It's about honest self-assessment. The Xcitizens who fixate on Keeper status during the Crucible are usually the ones who struggle. The ones who focus on operating well — and let the determination take care of itself — are the ones who thrive.

**Don't chase Keeper. Chase execution.** The determination follows.

## THE RULES OF SELF-TRACKING

1. **Be honest, not kind.** Inflating your scores doesn't help you. The facilitators have their own data. The value of this tracker is in the alignment (or misalignment) between your self-perception and reality.
2. **Track daily, not weekly.** Memory degrades. Write your scores the same evening, during the reflection period.
3. **Look for patterns, not isolated events.** One bad day is data. Three bad days is a pattern. One strong scenario is a signal. A week of strong scenarios is a trend.
4. **Use the tracker to prepare, not to perform.** This is a tool for your development. It's not graded. It's not shared (except with facilitators, who use it as one input among many). Use it to know yourself better.
