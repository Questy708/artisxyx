# 42. MISSION CARDS

**xHansa Fellowship — Crucible Mission Cards**
**Scenario Briefings for the 8-Week Crucible**

---

## WHAT MISSION CARDS ARE

Mission Cards are the scenario briefings you'll receive during the Crucible. Each card describes a scenario you and/or your Pod must execute. The cards are deliberately ambiguous — they give you a goal, a constraint, and incomplete information. What you do with it is the data.

**These are samples.** The actual cards you receive during your Crucible will be different (variation prevents "studying"). But the structure and the Archetype signals they surface are consistent.

---

## SAMPLE MISSION CARD 1 — "THE WRECKAGE"

**Delivered: Week 1, Day 2**
**Type: Individual + Pod**
**Duration: 3 hours**

---

### THE BRIEFING

You arrive at your Pod's workspace. On the table is a system — a physical apparatus (or a simulated digital system, depending on your Field). It is broken. It does not work.

**Your goal:** Make it work.

**Constraints:**
- You have 3 hours.
- You have the tools in the workspace and the materials in the Commons.
- You do NOT have: a manual, a schematic, or instructions.
- An "urgent" update will arrive every 30 minutes from an assessor (role-player). You must acknowledge each update within 5 minutes or face a penalty.

**Additional information:** None.

**Begin.**

---

### WHAT THIS CARD TESTS

| Archetype | Signal |
|-----------|--------|
| Pilot | Steps back, sees the system, prioritizes the fix sequence |
| Builder | Dives in, starts fixing, gets hands dirty |
| Operator | Keeps the system running (or the Pod organized) while others fix |
| Tracker | Identifies the root cause before anyone else |
| Hustler | Manages the interruptions, keeps the "assessor" calm |
| Comms | Translates the problem to the assessor clearly |

### FACILITATOR NOTES (NOT ON THE CARD)
- The system has 3 distinct faults. Only one is the root cause; the others are symptoms.
- The "urgent" updates are designed to interrupt flow. They are NOT actually urgent.
- Pods that try to fix all three faults simultaneously usually fail. Pods that diagnose first, then fix, usually succeed.
- The time pressure is real but not impossible. Pods that panic fail; Pods that execute calmly succeed.

## SAMPLE MISSION CARD 2 — "THE DEAL"

**Delivered: Week 2, Day 3**
**Type: Individual**
**Duration: 45 minutes**

---

### THE BRIEFING

You are seated across from a role-player. They are a decision-maker at an institution you need something from (a permit, a partnership, a resource — the specifics are in the sealed envelope in front of you).

**Your goal:** Secure their commitment.

**Constraints:**
- You have 45 minutes.
- The role-player is evasive, time-constrained, and will raise objections.
- You do NOT know their real constraint (the thing that would actually make them say yes).
- You may NOT offer anything you don't have authority to offer.

**Begin. Open the envelope.**

---

### WHAT THIS CARD TESTS

| Archetype | Signal |
|-----------|--------|
| Hustler | Closes the deal (or gets close) |
| Pilot | Reframes the negotiation to change the terms |
| Comms | Makes the complex ask legible |
| Operator | Manages the follow-up, the timeline, the logistics |
| Tracker | Identifies the role-player's real constraint |
| Builder | Offers to build a prototype to seal it |

### FACILITATOR NOTES
- The role-player's "real constraint" is hidden in their briefing. It's discoverable through active listening — but most Xcitizens are too busy pitching to listen.
- Xcitizens who offer things they don't have authority to offer are flagged (this is a deployment risk).
- The best performers ask questions before pitching. The worst performers pitch immediately.

## SAMPLE MISSION CARD 3 — "THE CROWD"

**Delivered: Week 3, Day 1**
**Type: Group (6–8 Xcitizens from different Pods)**
**Duration: 30 minutes**

---

### THE BRIEFING

You are placed in a group of 6–8 Xcitizens. You do not know each other (different Pods). No leader is designated.

**Your goal:** Produce a deployable plan for [problem stated on the card] in 30 minutes.

**Constraints:**
- 30 minutes. No extensions.
- The plan must be specific (not a vision statement — a deployable plan with steps, owners, and timeline).
- You must present the plan to a facilitator at the end. The facilitator will ask: "Who did what? Who led? Who tracked? Who built? Who closed? Who translated?"

**Begin.**

---

### WHAT THIS CARD TESTS

| Archetype | Signal |
|-----------|--------|
| Pilot | Emerges as leader (or sets direction without formal authority) |
| Builder | Starts constructing the plan/solution |
| Hustler | Drives consensus, closes disagreements |
| Operator | Keeps the group on time, on task |
| Tracker | Spots the flaw in the plan |
| Comms | Synthesizes and presents to the facilitator |

### FACILITATOR NOTES
- This is a leaderless group exercise. Leadership emerges — it is not assigned.
- The best groups self-organize within 5 minutes. The worst groups spend 15 minutes "deciding how to decide."
- The facilitator's question at the end ("Who did what?") is the data. Xcitizens who claim credit for everything are flagged. Xcitizens who accurately describe their role and others' roles are noted positively.

## SAMPLE MISSION CARD 4 — "THE WALL" (WEEKS 4–5)

**Delivered: Week 4, Day 1 (start of the Wall)**
**Type: Continuous (10–14 days)**
**Duration: Sustained**

---

### THE BRIEFING

For the next 10–14 days, you will operate under sustained cognitive load. Scenarios will be given to you one after another, with no meaningful breaks. Sleep will be restricted to 5–6 hours per night.

**Your goal:** Continue operating.

**Constraints:**
- You will receive a new scenario every 2–4 hours.
- Scenarios will be complex and ambiguous (same as The Shock, but sustained).
- You will not be told when the Wall ends.
- You may not quit during the Wall. If you say "I want to quit," the response will be: "Noted. We'll talk after the Wall. For now, execute."

**Additional information:** The medical team is monitoring your Neural Link data continuously. If they determine you need rest, they will pull you. This is not a failure — it is safety.

**Begin. The first scenario is on your table.**

---

### WHAT THIS CARD TESTS

This is the richest data collection period of the Crucible. By the end of the Wall, Archetype assignments are confirmed:

| Archetype | Signal (observed as the Xcitizen fatigues) |
|-----------|---------------------------------------------|
| Operator | Keeps executing when spent |
| Pilot | Starts cutting scope to ship |
| Builder | Keeps building despite fatigue |
| Tracker | Finds the shortcut |
| Hustler | Reframes the problem to make it solvable |
| Comms | Documents so others can continue |

### FACILITATOR NOTES
- The Wall is the most important data collection period. Pay close attention.
- The well-rested Xcitizen of Week 1 may be a different person than the exhausted Xcitizen of Week 5. The Week 5 data is the ground-truth.
- Do NOT ease up. The Wall's value is in its relentlessness.
- Coordinate with the medical team continuously. They have override authority.

## SAMPLE MISSION CARD 5 — "THE DEBRIEF" (WEEK 8, GATE 0)

**Delivered: Week 8, Day 3**
**Type: Individual**
**Duration: 15 minutes**

---

### THE BRIEFING

You are seated with a facilitator. No scenario. No task.

**Your goal:** Answer honestly.

**Questions:**
1. "What happened during the Crucible?"
2. "What did you do?"
3. "What should you have done differently?"
4. "What did you learn about yourself?"
5. "Are you ready to deploy?"

**Begin.**

---

### WHAT THIS CARD TESTS

This is the self-awareness test — a Gate 0 criterion. The facilitator is assessing:
- Accurate self-assessment (does the Xcitizen's account match the facilitator's observations?)
- Blaming the scenario/tools/others (red flag)
- Ability to articulate what they'd change (growth signal)
- Honesty about readiness (claiming readiness when clearly not ready is a red flag; honest uncertainty is a positive signal)

### FACILITATOR NOTES
- This is the most predictive single assessment for deployment success.
- Xcitizens who say "I should have done X differently" and can articulate why, are almost always ready.
- Xcitizens who say "I did great" or blame external factors are almost always not ready.
- The question "Are you ready to deploy?" is a trap. The right answer is honest — "Yes, because..." or "I'm not sure, because..." The wrong answer is an unqualified "Yes" without evidence.

## HOW TO READ A MISSION CARD

When you receive a Mission Card during the Crucible:

1. **Read it fully.** Every word matters. Missing a constraint is a failure.
2. **Identify the goal.** What are you actually being asked to do?
3. **Identify the constraints.** What can't you do? What don't you have?
4. **Note what's missing.** The card deliberately omits information. What do you need to figure out?
5. **Act.** Don't deliberate. Execute. Adjust based on what happens.
6. **Report.** Tell your Pod (or the facilitator) what you're doing and why. Communication is data.
7. **Reflect.** In your evening journal, write about what happened. Be honest.

---

## THE RULES OF MISSION CARDS

1. **You may not ask the facilitator for clarification.** The ambiguity is the test.
2. **You may not consult external resources** (internet, books, other Pods) unless the card explicitly permits it.
3. **You may use the Commons** (League IP repository) unless the card prohibits it.
4. **You may collaborate with your Pod** unless the card specifies "individual."
5. **Time limits are real.** When time is up, it's up. No extensions.
6. **The card is the card.** You may not renegotiate the terms.
