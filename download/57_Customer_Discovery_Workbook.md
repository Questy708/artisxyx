## PURPOSE

Customer Discovery is the process of understanding your target users before you build for them. It happens continuously — starting in Q1 (before the Minimal Version) and continuing through Q3 (replication in new geographies).

**The rule:** You do NOT build the Minimal Version until you've completed 25+ customer discovery interviews. Building without discovery is guessing. The Gate System doesn't reward guessing.

This workbook defines the interview process, templates, and synthesis method.

---

## 1. THE INTERVIEW PROCESS

### 1.1 Who to Interview
- **Target users:** The people who will actually use your ProtoCo
- **Target buyers:** The people who will pay (may be different from users)
- **Adjacent stakeholders:** Distributors, community leaders, regulators, suppliers
- **Anchor Partner representatives:** The people at your Anchor Partner who will sign the LOI

### 1.2 How Many
- **Q1 (before Minimal Version build):** 25+ interviews minimum
- **Q1 (during build, after initial 25):** Ongoing — 5+ per week
- **Q2 (revenue phase):** 10+ per month (customers and churned customers)
- **Q3 (replication):** 15+ per new geography (before deploying)

### 1.3 Interview Format
- **Duration:** 30–45 minutes
- **Setting:** In-person (preferred) or video call
- **Recording:** Audio record (with permission) for accuracy. Take notes during.
- **Participants:** 1 interviewee at a time (not groups — you want individual perspectives)

### Pre-Interview

```
**Date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]

**Geography:** [City, Country]

```

### Interview Questions (Adapt to Your ProtoCo)

**Section A: Current Behavior (understand the status quo)**
1. "Walk me through a typical day. Where does [problem area] come up?"
2. "What are you currently doing about [problem]?"
3. "How much time/money/effort does that take?"
4. "What's the hardest part about [current solution]?"
5. "When was the last time you [relevant action]? Tell me about it."

**Section B: Pain Points (understand what hurts)**
6. "What frustrates you most about [current situation]?"
7. "If you could change one thing about [problem area], what would it be?"
8. "What happens when [current solution] fails? What's the impact?"
9. "Who else is affected when this goes wrong?"

**Section C: Willingness to Pay (understand the economics)**
10. "How much do you currently spend on [current solution]?"
11. "If there were a better solution, what would it be worth to you?"
12. "How would you pay for something like that? (Cash, mobile money, subscription, pay-per-use?)"
13. "Who makes the purchasing decision? (You, someone else?)"

**Section D: Solution Reaction (test your concept — Q1 only, after initial 15 interviews)**
14. "If there were a [describe your solution in one sentence], would that be useful?"
15. "What would need to be true for you to use it?"
16. "What concerns would you have?"
17. "Who else do you think would use this?"

**Section E: Logistics (understand deployment constraints)**
18. "How do you currently access [relevant infrastructure — power, internet, water, transport]?"
19. "What would it take for you to adopt a new [solution type]?"
20. "Is there anyone I should talk to who deals with this differently?"

## 3. INTERVIEW RULES

### 3.1 Don't Pitch
The interview is about THEM, not YOU. Do not describe your solution until Section D (and only after the first 15 interviews). If you pitch first, they'll tell you what you want to hear, not what they actually think.

### 3.2 Ask Open Questions
"Tell me about..." not "Do you think...?" Open questions reveal what they actually care about. Closed questions confirm your assumptions (which may be wrong).

### 3.3 Listen More Than You Talk
If you're talking more than 30% of the interview, you're doing it wrong. Let them talk. Let them go off on tangents — that's where the insights are.

### 3.4 Don't Lead the Witness
"This would save you time, right?" → Leading. "How would this affect your time?" → Open. Don't put words in their mouth.

### 3.5 Take Notes, Don't Rely on Memory
Audio record (with permission). Take notes. Write up the key insights within 1 hour of the interview — memory degrades fast.

### 3.6 Be Honest About What You Hear
If 20 out of 25 interviews say your solution won't work, that's the data. Don't spin it. Take it to the Pilot. Adjust the solution. The Gate System will find out the truth eventually — better to find it in interview than in the field.

## 5. THE 25-INTERVIEW GATE (INTERNAL)

Before building the Minimal Version, the Pod must complete 25+ customer discovery interviews and synthesize the findings. This is an internal gate (not a Strategic Command Gate) — but the Pod Leader will not approve the build plan without it.

### Evidence Required
- 25+ completed interview notes (in Notion)
- 5+ synthesis documents (1 per 5 interviews)
- A "Customer Personas" document (2-3 personas derived from the interviews)
- A "Willingness to Pay" assessment

### What Happens If the Interviews Say "Don't Build This"
If 15+ of 25 interviews indicate the solution won't work:
1. The Pod does NOT build the Minimal Version yet.
2. The Pilot escalates to the Pod Leader.
3. The Pod Leader may escalate to Strategic Command.
4. Options: pivot the solution, adjust the target geography, or (rare) request a commission change.
5. **Better to find out at interview 25 than at Gate 1.** The interviews are the early warning system.

## 7. Q2 AND Q3 CUSTOMER DISCOVERY

### Q2 (Revenue Phase)
- Interview paying customers: Why did you buy? How is it working?
- Interview churned customers: Why did you leave? What went wrong?
- Interview non-customers: Why haven't you bought? What's the barrier?

### Q3 (Replication Phase)
- Interview target users in each new geography BEFORE deploying
- Adapt the interview guide for local context (language, culture, market structure)
- 15+ interviews per new geography
- The goal: confirm the model transfers before you invest in replication
