# 46. SIMULATION MANUAL

**xHansa Fellowship — Crucible Simulation Manual**
**INTERNAL — CRUCIBLE FACILITATORS ONLY**

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This manual defines the scenario simulations used in the Crucible — their design principles, the scenario library structure, the run protocols, and the assessment integration. It is the facilitator's operational reference for running simulations.

---

## 2. SIMULATION DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### 2.1 Ambiguity is the Test
Simulations never give clear instructions. They give a goal, a constraint, and incomplete information. Clear instructions test compliance; ambiguity tests execution. If a Xcitizen can succeed by following instructions, the simulation has failed.

### 2.2 Pressure Reveals Truth
Time pressure is not cruelty — it is data collection. Well-rested Xcitizens perform. Exhausted Xcitizens reveal who they actually are. The pressure is the point. But: pressure must be calibrated. The medical team monitors for dangerous physiological responses and has override authority.

### 2.3 Shift the Ground
Mid-simulation, change something. A constraint is lifted. A resource is removed. A new "urgent" demand is introduced. This tests adaptability — the most critical deployment skill. A Xcitizen who can execute a plan but can't adapt when the plan breaks is not deployment-ready.

### 2.4 Interrupt Deliberately
Use assessor role-players to interrupt with "urgent" updates. This tests prioritization — does the Xcitizen drop everything for the interruption, or do they manage it? The interruptions are NOT actually urgent (the facilitator knows this; the Xcitizen does not).

### 2.5 Never Lead
Facilitators observe. They do not step in to "help" a struggling Pod. They do not give hints. They do not redirect. If a Pod fails a simulation, that is the data. Record it. Move on.

### 2.6 Safety First
Simulations are intense, but they are never unsafe. If Neural Link data shows a dangerous physiological response, the medical team pulls the Xcitizen. The facilitator does not override the medical team. Ever.

### 2.7 Variation
No two cohorts receive the same simulation sequence. Variation prevents "studying" for the Crucible (which is impossible by design, but predictability undermines the data). The scenario library is large; facilitators select and remix for each cohort.

## 3. THE SCENARIO LIBRARY

The League maintains a scenario library in the Commons (facilitator access). Scenarios are categorized by:

### 3.1 By Movement
- **Shock scenarios** (Weeks 1–5): Ambiguous, high-pressure, shifting, designed to strip compliance conditioning.
- **Wall scenarios** (Weeks 4–5): Same as Shock but sustained — complex, relentless, designed to reveal behavioral ground-truth under exhaustion.
- **Reconstruction scenarios** (Weeks 6–7): ProtoCo-adjacent, designed to prepare for deployment. Less pressure, more skill-building.

### 3.2 By Archetype Signal
Each scenario is tagged with the primary Archetype signals it surfaces:
- **Pilot signals:** Scenarios requiring prioritization, scope-cutting, reframing
- **Builder signals:** Scenarios requiring construction, prototyping, hands-on building
- **Hustler signals:** Scenarios requiring negotiation, stakeholder management, closing
- **Operator signals:** Scenarios requiring system maintenance, timeline management, composure
- **Tracker signals:** Scenarios requiring diagnosis, risk-spotting, information-finding
- **Comms signals:** Scenarios requiring synthesis, documentation, presentation

Most scenarios surface multiple Archetype signals — that's the point. Pods need all six.

### 3.3 By Field
Some scenarios are Field-specific (a Water Systems scenario for Pods deployed in Water). These are used in Reconstruction (Weeks 6–7) to begin ProtoCo preparation. Shock and Wall scenarios are Field-agnostic (they test behavior, not domain knowledge).

### 3.4 Versioning
Scenarios are versioned (v1.0, v1.1, v2.0). Versions evolve based on:
- Deployment data (which scenarios predicted deployment success/failure?)
- Facilitator feedback (which scenarios produced clean data vs. noisy data?)
- Xcitizen feedback (post-Crucible debriefs — which scenarios felt relevant?)

Major version changes require Strategic Command approval.

## 4. THE FIVE CORE SIMULATION TYPES

### 4.1 The Wreckage (Diagnostic + Build)
**Tests:** Builder, Tracker, Pilot, Operator
**Structure:** A broken system (physical or digital) must be diagnosed and fixed under time pressure, with interruptions.
**See:** Document 42, Mission Card 1

### 4.2 The Deal (Negotiation + Closing)
**Tests:** Hustler, Comms, Pilot, Tracker
**Structure:** A role-player negotiation. The Xcitizen must secure a commitment from an evasive, time-constrained counterpart with a hidden real constraint.
**See:** Document 42, Mission Card 2

### 4.3 The Crowd (Leaderless Group)
**Tests:** All six (emergent roles)
**Structure:** 6–8 Xcitizens from different Pods, no designated leader, must produce a deployable plan in 30 minutes. Roles emerge organically.
**See:** Document 42, Mission Card 3

### 4.4 The Wall (Sustained Cognitive Load)
**Tests:** All six (under exhaustion — the ground-truth)
**Structure:** 10–14 days of relentless scenarios, 5–6 hours of sleep per night, minimal breaks. The richest data collection period.
**See:** Document 42, Mission Card 4

### 4.5 The Debrief (Self-Awareness)
**Tests:** Self-awareness (a Gate 0 criterion)
**Structure:** Individual, 15 minutes. Five questions. The facilitator assesses alignment between the Xcitizen's self-perception and observed behavior.
**See:** Document 42, Mission Card 5

## 5. RUNNING A SIMULATION — PROTOCOL

### 5.1 Pre-Simulation (Facilitator Prep, 30 min before)
1. **Select the scenario** from the library (per the cohort's scenario plan).
2. **Prepare the materials:** scenario card, props (if physical), role-player briefings (if applicable).
3. **Brief the role-players** (assessors, simulated partners). They must know their role, their hidden constraints, and their scripts — but they must NOT know the Archetype assessment criteria (prevents biasing their performance).
4. **Set up the space:** Pod workspace, Commons, or scenario-specific location.
5. **Coordinate with medical team:** confirm Neural Link monitoring is active for all participating Xcitizens.

### 5.2 Simulation Launch (Minute 0)
1. **Distribute the Mission Card** (or brief verbally — never both; pick one).
2. **State the time limit.** Start the clock.
3. **Step back.** Observe. Do not intervene.
4. **If interruptions are part of the scenario,** deploy role-players at the scheduled intervals. Role-players must stay in character — do not break character to "help."

### 5.3 During the Simulation
- **Observe continuously** using the six-Archetype framework (Document 45).
- **Score in real-time** (0–5 per Archetype, per Xcitizen). Don't wait until the end — memory degrades.
- **Note specific behaviors** (not just scores): "Xcitizen A dove in without diagnosing — Builder signal, but risk pattern." "Xcitizen B kept the group on time — Operator signal, strong."
- **Do NOT intervene** unless there's a safety issue (medical team handles safety).
- **If a Pod is failing,** let it fail. Failure is data. Record what happened, how they handled it, and what they did next.

### 5.4 Post-Simulation (Debrief, 30–60 min)
1. **Cohort or Pod debrief:** "What happened? What did you do? What should you have done differently?"
2. **Ask, don't tell.** Let the Xcitizens reflect. Your job is to surface self-awareness, not to lecture.
3. **Note self-awareness** (a Gate 0 criterion): Does the Xcitizen's account match your observation? Alignment = positive signal. Misalignment = data.
4. **Submit scores** to the assessment system within 1 hour of the debrief.
5. **Sync with co-facilitators** (15 min): compare scores, flag discrepancies, calibrate.

## 6. ROLE-PLAYER MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Who Are the Role-Players?
- League staff trained in role-play
- Senior Xcitizens (post-deployment) who volunteer
- Professional role-actors (for high-stakes simulations like The Deal)

### 6.2 Role-Player Briefing
Role-players receive:
- Their character brief (who they are, what they want, what they're hiding)
- The scenario constraints (what they can and cannot do)
- The time limit
- The instruction: "Stay in character. Do not break character to help. Do not reveal the assessment criteria."

### 6.3 Role-Player Conduct
- Role-players must NOT know the Archetype assessment criteria (prevents biasing toward/against specific Xcitizens).
- Role-players must NOT deviate from their brief (consistency is essential for clean data).
- Role-players must NOT socialize with Xcitizens outside of simulations (maintains professional distance).

## 7. THE WALL — SPECIAL SIMULATION PROTOCOLS

### 7.1 Intensification Rules
- Scenarios are longer (4+ hours per block, vs. 2–3 hours in normal weeks).
- Breaks are shorter (5–20 min, vs. 15–30 min in normal weeks).
- Sleep is restricted (5–6 hours, vs. 7–8 in normal weeks).
- The pace is relentless — scenarios begin immediately after the previous one ends.

### 7.2 Medical Coordination
- The medical team monitors Neural Link data 24/7 during the Wall.
- The medical team has **override authority** — they can pull any Xcitizen for mandatory rest at any time.
- Facilitators do NOT override medical pulls. The Wall is intense; it is not unsafe.
- Medical pulls are NOT a Gate 0 failure. The Xcitizen returns to the Crucible after recovery.

### 7.3 No-Quit Rule
- During the Wall, if a Xcitizen says "I want to quit," the facilitator response is: "Noted. We'll talk after the Wall. For now, execute."
- The Wall is the worst moment. Decisions made during it are not reliable.
- If the Xcitizen insists on quitting, they are pulled from scenarios and meet with Strategic Command (not a facilitator) to discuss.

### 7.4 Facilitator Self-Care
- Facilitators also experience the Wall's intensity (you're observing 14+ hour days).
- Rotate facilitator shifts during the Wall (no facilitator runs more than 3 consecutive Wall days without a rest day).
- The medical team monitors facilitator Neural Link data too. You are not exempt.

## 8. ASSESSMENT INTEGRATION

### 8.1 Real-Time Scoring
Facilitators score in real-time during simulations (0–5 per Archetype per Xcitizen per scenario). Scores are submitted to the assessment system within 1 hour of each debrief.

### 8.2 Aggregation
The assessment system aggregates scores across:
- All scenarios (Shock, Wall, Reconstruction)
- All facilitators (3+ per Xcitizen)
- All 8 weeks

The aggregate determines the primary and secondary Archetype (see Document 45).

### 8.3 Gate 0 Integration
Simulation scores are one of four Gate 0 criteria:
1. Attendance (objective)
2. Behavioral certification (facilitator aggregate — includes simulation scores)
3. Archetype stability (simulation score clarity — is there a 3+ point lead?)
4. Physical/cognitive recovery (Neural Link data from the medical team)

### 8.4 Post-Crucible Validation
After each cohort, Strategic Command reviews:
- Which simulation scores correlated with deployment success (Gate 1–4 passes)?
- Which simulation scores correlated with Kill Switch events?
- Which scenarios were most predictive?
This data feeds into scenario library updates and facilitator recalibration.

## 9. SCENARIO CONFIDENTIALITY

### 9.1 What's Confidential
- Scenario details (goals, constraints, solutions)
- Role-player briefings and hidden constraints
- Facilitator scoring and assessment discussions
- Gate 0 deliberations

### 9.2 Why It Matters
- If Xcitizens know the scenario in advance, they can prepare (which defeats the purpose — the Crucible tests un-preparable-for behavior).
- If scenario details leak to future cohorts, the data is contaminated.
- Facilitators who leak scenario details face Conduct review.

### 9.3 Xcitizen Debriefs
- Xcitizens may discuss their own experience in general terms ("I faced a scenario about a broken system") but must NOT share specific details that would compromise the scenario for future cohorts.
- This is a confidentiality obligation that survives the Crucible.

---

## 10. THE SCENARIO DEVELOPMENT PROCESS

### 10.1 New Scenario Proposal
Any facilitator can propose a new scenario. Proposals include:
- Scenario goal and structure
- Archetype signals it surfaces
- Logistics (materials, role-players, time required)
- Expected data quality (will it produce clean signals or noise?)

### 10.2 Review
New scenarios are reviewed by the Scenario Committee (senior facilitators + Strategic Command). Approved scenarios are added to the library as v1.0 and piloted in the next cohort.

### 10.3 Post-Pilot Review
After piloting, the scenario is reviewed:
- Did it produce clean data?
- Did Xcitizens find it relevant (post-Crucible debrief)?
- Did it correlate with deployment outcomes?
If yes, it's retained. If no, it's revised or retired.
